Dimensional analysis is a mathematical method used to convert a quantity from one unit of measurement to another. It’s an art thief swapping the bag of sand for the cursed gold idol. The secret weapon is the conversion factor, which is a ratio expressing how many of one unit are equal to another unit.

Why doesn't the alarm go off when we swap the units? Because the numerator and denominator of a valid conversion factor represent the exact same physical quantity! One foot is the exact same slice of reality as twelve inches. Since they are physically identical, multiplying any measurement by a valid conversion factor is mathematically equivalent to multiplying the measurement by the number one. The size of the object hasn't changed; it just put on a disguise!

Here is the trick: in dimensional analysis calculations, units are treated as algebraic variables. They are literally just $x$'s and $y$'s wearing trench coats! And because of that, units present in both the numerator and denominator of a dimensional analysis expression cancel each other out algebraically. Put "inches" on top and "inches" on the bottom? Assassin's creed—they eliminate each other!
