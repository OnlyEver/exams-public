Now, let's talk about what happens when our A-lister, Light, actually interacts with the paparazzi. When light hits a surface it can't pass through, it throws a petty, perfectly symmetrical fit. Reflection occurs when a light wave bounces off a surface. The universe demands absolute geometric respect here. The law of reflection states that the angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection. If light hits a surface at a harsh 45-degree angle, it will bounce back at that exact same 45-degree angle. It is the ultimate visual clapback! This perfectly preserved bounce is exactly why mirrors utilize reflection to form visual images. You look into the mirror, the light bounces off your face, hits the glass, and snaps back at the exact same angle so you can see your own gorgeous, terrified reflection.

Refraction occurs due to a change in the speed of light as the wave enters a new medium! We actually exploit this swerving cart phenomenon all the time. Lenses utilize refraction to bend light for magnifying or reducing images, literally steering the light right into our eyeballs.
