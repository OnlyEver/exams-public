Listen to me! Stop looking at your phone and look at this wet napkin! You think the reality TV show you’re watching has drama? The real, unhinged, high-stakes drama is happening right here. In this bar, in your glass, in the very air we are breathing! Because matter is defined as anything that has mass and takes up space. If you can weigh it and it takes up room in this universe, it is officially a cast member on the most chaotic show in existence. 

And the cast? All matter is composed of tiny fundamental particles called atoms. They are the absolute, undisputed divas of reality. An atom is the smallest unit of an element that retains the chemical properties of that element. But you have to zoom in to see the club layout. Think of the atom like the most exclusive, high-security VIP nightclub. Right in the middle, you've got the ultra-exclusive VIP room—the nucleus. The nucleus is the small, dense, positively charged center of an atom. Who gets to sit in the VIP room? The absolute heavyweights. Protons are located inside the nucleus of an atom, and neutrons are located inside the nucleus of an atom. A proton is a subatomic particle with a positive electrical charge—they bring the hyped-up, positive club vibes. A neutron is a subatomic particle with no electrical charge—they are literally just sitting there sipping water, totally neutral, soaking up the ambiance. But do not mess with them, because they are thick! Protons have a mass of approximately one atomic mass unit, and neutrons have a mass of approximately one atomic mass unit. Because these guys are built like bouncers, most of the mass of an atom is concentrated in the nucleus!

Meanwhile, outside the VIP ropes, you've got the frantic paparazzi. An electron is a subatomic particle with a negative electrical charge. Always negative, always bringing the drama! Electrons orbit the nucleus of an atom in regions called electron clouds or shells. They are running laps around the club! And they are scrawny—the mass of an electron is roughly 1/1836th the mass of a proton. You couldn't even feel one if it slammed right into your shin. Because these tiny, negative paparazzi are running in massive outer circles around the VIP room, most of the volume of an atom consists of empty space in the electron cloud. This solid wooden bar we're leaning on? It’s mostly empty space!

How do we tell these microscopic nightclubs apart? The bouncers check the ultimate VIP list. The atomic number of an element is equal to the number of protons in the atoms of that element. Period. You cannot fake it. The number of protons determines the elemental identity of an atom. If the proton count changes, the club completely rebrands! But what about the overall head count inside? The mass number of an atom is the sum of the protons and neutrons in the atom. Sometimes, the exact same club has a different number of neutral freeloaders sitting inside. Isotopes are atoms of the same element that have different numbers of neutrons. It's the same club, same identity, just carrying different baggage! And what if the paparazzi out front get wild? Ions are atoms that have gained or lost electrons to acquire a net electrical charge. They either kidnapped some negative paparazzi from another club, or lost their own to the tabloids!

Now, let's talk about relationship status. An element is a pure substance consisting of only one type of atom. They are fiercely independent singles. Pure! Untethered! Gold is an example of an element. A solid bar of gold is just billions of gold atoms screaming, "I don't need anybody to make me shine!" But nature hates a vacuum, and it loves a deeply transformative, codependent marriage. A compound is a pure substance formed by the chemical combination of two or more different elements in fixed proportions. They get hitched, and it changes them entirely. Compounds have different physical and chemical properties than their constituent elements. Look at the water in your glass! Water is a compound made of hydrogen and oxygen. Listen to me: hydrogen alone will literally explode. Oxygen alone fuels raging fires. But they sign a marriage certificate in a strict two-to-one ratio, and suddenly they turn into a liquid that we use to put out fires! Marriage changes people, man!

How do we judge these singles and couples? We stalk their profiles. We look at their properties. A physical property is a characteristic of a substance that can be observed without changing the chemical identity of the substance. It's totally superficial! You don't have to break up a marriage to see it. Mass is a physical property measuring the amount of matter in an object. How heavy is their emotional baggage? Volume is a physical property measuring the amount of three-dimensional space an object occupies. How much room do they take up on the couch? Density is a physical property defined as the mass per unit volume of a substance. How tightly packed is their nonsense? Even their outfit—color is classified as a physical property! We can also measure their physical breaking points. The melting point is the specific temperature at which a solid becomes a liquid. The boiling point is the specific temperature at which a liquid becomes a gas. And let's not forget how they handle a spark. Electrical conductivity is a physical property describing how well a substance allows electricity to flow through the substance. 

But then... there are the dark traits. The massive red flags. A chemical property describes the ability of a substance to undergo a specific chemical change to form a new substance. This is what happens when they finally snap and enter the witness protection program as a totally new person! Flammability is a chemical property indicating how easily a substance can catch fire. Reactivity is a chemical property describing how readily a substance undergoes a chemical reaction with another substance. Do they start swinging the minute they bump into someone at the bar? Toxicity is a chemical property describing the degree to which a substance can harm living organisms. Straight up toxic personalities!

Because of this, you have to know the difference between a superficial makeover and total identity theft. A physical change alters the form or appearance of a substance without creating a new substance. It’s just a new haircut! Tearing a piece of paper is a physical change—it’s shredded, but it’s still paper. Dissolving salt into water is a physical change—the salt is still right there, just hiding in the water! And write this on your hand: changes of state are always classified as physical changes. Always! Melting ice into liquid water is a physical change. It’s still H2O, it just took its winter coat off!

But a chemical change? That’s scorched earth. A chemical change results in the formation of one or more new substances with different properties. There is no hitting the undo button. The rusting of iron is a chemical change. Burning wood is a chemical change. Baking a cake is a chemical change—you can't un-bake the eggs out of a cake! How do we know the ultimate drama went down? Nature leaves receipts. The production of a new gas during a reaction often indicates a chemical change—suddenly they are blowing hot air and fizzing! A sudden unexpected change in color during a reaction can indicate a chemical change. The formation of a solid precipitate from two liquid solutions indicates a chemical change—that’s like mixing two perfectly clear drinks and suddenly a powdery brick falls out of them! And finally, the release or absorption of heat without an external source indicates a chemical change. If you mix two things and the glass gets burning hot or freezing cold all by itself, they are fighting on a molecular level!

But here is the most insane rule of the entire show. The producers enforce it with a strict iron fist. The Law of Conservation of Mass states that matter is neither created nor destroyed during an ordinary chemical reaction. No one leaves the show, they just change outfits and swap partners! Because of this, the total mass of the reactants in a chemical reaction exactly equals the total mass of the products. Every single dramatic atom is accounted for!

Now, let's hit the dance floor. The energy of the club determines how the atoms act. Adding thermal energy to a substance increases the kinetic energy of the particles in the substance. Heat them up, they go wild! Removing thermal energy from a substance decreases the kinetic energy of the particles in the substance. Cool them down, they freeze up in panic. This creates the four fundamental states of matter: solid, liquid, gas, and plasma.

When it’s early in the night, everyone is awkward. A solid has a definite shape. A solid has a definite volume. The particles in a solid are tightly packed together in fixed positions. They are just vibrating in the corner, holding their drinks, trapped in place! But then the DJ turns it up. Melting is the phase transition from a solid to a liquid. Now we're grooving! A liquid has a definite volume, but a liquid takes the shape of the container holding the liquid. The particles in a liquid are close together, but the particles in a liquid can slide past one another freely. They are mingling! (Of course, if someone kills the vibe, they lock back up—freezing is the phase transition from a liquid to a solid).

Then, the beat drops. Total chaos. Vaporization is the phase transition from a liquid to a gas. If it's just the folks near the door sneaking out to get tacos, evaporation is a type of vaporization that occurs only on the surface of a liquid. But if the whole club loses its collective mind at once, boiling is a type of vaporization that occurs throughout the entire volume of a liquid! Now they are a gas! A gas does not have a definite shape. A gas does not have a definite volume. The particles in a gas are widely spaced, and the particles in a gas move rapidly in all directions. Absolute mosh pit! (If they get tired and sweat on the walls, condensation is the phase transition from a gas to a liquid).

Sometimes, people skip the mingling entirely. Sublimation is the phase transition directly from a solid to a gas without passing through the liquid phase. You ever see someone go from standing awkwardly stiff at the bar to sprinting out the door into the night without a word? That's dry ice! Dry ice undergoes sublimation at room temperature. Spooky fog, no liquid! The exact opposite? Deposition is the phase transition directly from a gas to a solid without passing through the liquid phase. Bam, slamming headfirst into a brick wall. The formation of frost on a cold window is an example of deposition. The vapor hits the cold glass and instantly turns to solid ice!

But what if the club gets struck by lighting? The absolute highest tier of chaotic energy! A plasma is a highly energized state of matter consisting of a gas of ions and free electrons. It’s a riot! Their electron clothes are literally ripped off! Lightning consists largely of plasma. And out in the vast universe? Stars consist largely of plasma. It is raw, unhinged, burning cosmic energy!

Finally, let's look at the crowd itself. Most of the time, reality isn't a pure solo act or a pure marriage. It's a messy group hang. A mixture is a combination of two or more substances that are not chemically combined. They are just sitting in the same booth. Because they aren't married or chemically bonded, each substance in a mixture retains its own individual chemical properties. And because they are just physically sitting near each other, mixtures can be separated by physical means. You can just drag them apart by the collar!

Sometimes the group is super cliquey. A heterogeneous mixture contains substances that are not evenly distributed throughout. A bowl of trail mix is an example of a heterogeneous mixture—you reach in, you get M&Ms one time, peanuts the next. Zero consistency! If the clique has really big, heavy egos that eventually crash from exhaustion, it's called a suspension. A suspension is a heterogeneous mixture containing large solid particles that will settle upon standing. Think of muddy water! Leave it alone, the heavy dirt gives up and sinks to the bottom.

But sometimes, the vibe is flawless and everyone blends perfectly into one seamless squad. A homogeneous mixture contains substances that are evenly distributed. Every sip is exactly the same because a homogeneous mixture appears uniform throughout. Another word for this perfect harmony? A solution. A solution is another name for a homogeneous mixture. In this beautiful blend, you have two roles. A solute is the substance that is dissolved in a solution. A solvent is the substance that does the dissolving in a solution. If you're drinking ocean water, in a saltwater solution, salt acts as the solute, and in a saltwater solution, water acts as the solvent. Water is so aggressively good at mingling that water is commonly referred to as the universal solvent. It’ll dissolve almost anything! And solutions don't just have to be wet! Hardcore metalheads can blend too. An alloy is a solid solution composed of two or more metals. Steel is an example of an alloy. Iron and carbon beautifully, flawlessly mixed in solid form!

But what if the drama gets too hot and we need to break up the friend group? We said mixtures can be separated physically! If we've got a cliquey, chunky mess, we hire a bouncer. Filtration is a separation method used to remove solid particles from a liquid in a heterogeneous mixture. Pour it through a filter, catch the big solid chunks, let the liquid run away! But what if they are perfectly blended in a homogeneous solution? We turn up the heat! Distillation is a separation method that separates homogeneous mixtures based on differences in boiling points. We boil the saltwater! The water can't take the heat, vaporizes into a gas, and runs away, leaving the solid salt crying alone in the pan!
