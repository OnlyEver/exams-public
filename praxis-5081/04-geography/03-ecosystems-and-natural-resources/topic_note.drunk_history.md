Two tyrannical administrators: Heat and Moisture. The spatial distribution of global biomes is primarily determined by temperature and precipitation patterns. It’s a ruthless biological equation. Take deforestation, which is the large-scale removal of forest biomes. This leads directly to soil erosion, which is the physical displacement of the upper, nutrient-rich layer of soil. Deforestation and unsustainable farming practices accelerate soil erosion, letting the wind and rain wash all the fertile dirt right down the drain. Do this enough in dry areas, and you trigger an absolute catastrophe: desertification, the process by which fertile land degrades into arid desert. Overgrazing and prolonged drought are primary causes of desertification. You let the cows eat the grass down to the nub, the rains fail, and boom—your farm is a sandbox. This isn't theoretical! The Sahel region of Africa experiences severe desertification due to overfarming and climatic changes, displacing millions of people. 

And the water supply? We are drinking the school dry. Water scarcity occurs when human demand for freshwater exceeds the available natural supply. Why do we do this? Why do we destroy our own campus? Because of the most tragic behavioral psychology concept ever: the tragedy of the commons! The tragedy of the commons describes a situation where individuals deplete a shared resource for short-term self-interest. It’s like leaving a massive bowl of premium candy in the teachers' lounge for the whole week. Everyone knows it should be rationed, but the gym teacher grabs two handfuls for himself, so the math teacher panics and takes three handfuls, and by 9:00 AM on Monday, the bowl is empty and everyone is mad! 

This exact candy bowl panic brings us to the janitor's supply closet. We have two types of supplies. Renewable natural resources can be replenished naturally at a rate comparable to their rate of human consumption. Stuff the universe just keeps delivering. Solar energy is a renewable natural resource. Wind energy is a renewable natural resource. Geothermal energy is a renewable natural resource. Biomass is a renewable natural resource. Hydropower is a renewable natural resource. As long as the sun shines, the earth boils, and the rivers flow, the delivery truck keeps arriving. 

But then we have the nonrenewable natural resources, which exist in finite quantities and cannot be rapidly replaced by natural processes. Once they are gone, the universe is not sending more. Coal is a nonrenewable natural resource. Petroleum is a nonrenewable natural resource. Natural gas is a nonrenewable natural resource. And uranium used for nuclear energy generation is a nonrenewable natural resource. Those first three? They are fossil fuels, which are formed from the fossilized remains of ancient plants and animals over millions of years. We are literally running our entire modern global economy by burning the compressed, prehistoric corpses of swamp ferns and plankton! But here’s the kicker that’s ruining the school: the combustion of fossil fuels releases carbon dioxide into the atmosphere. We are basically hotboxing the entire biosphere! Increased atmospheric carbon dioxide contributes to the greenhouse effect and global climate change, trapping heat and completely rewriting the temperature and precipitation rules that govern every single biome. 

To stop the school from completely burning down, the administration has to step in with policy, and they basically argue over two different management philosophies. Conservation involves the sustainable management and protection of natural resources for continued human use. It’s the principal saying, "You can cut down some trees for paper, but you have to plant new ones and you can't touch the owl nests." Preservation, on the other hand, involves protecting natural areas from any human use or alteration. It’s padlocking the music room and saying, "This forest is pristine wilderness, nobody is allowed to build a road, mine a rock, or ride a four-wheeler in here, ever." Ultimately, the modern goal of humanity has to be sustainable development, a framework that meets current human needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs. It means letting the seniors have a great prom without bankrupting the freshman class.
