Imagine two [architects](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Architect) forced to inhabit the same planetary real estate, each holding a [detonator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Detonator) to a [bomb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bomb) buried beneath the floorboards, fighting a decades-long battle over the structural foundation of the house. **The [Cold War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cold_War) was an era of intense [geopolitical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geopolitics) and ideological rivalry between the [capitalist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capitalism) [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States) and the [communist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communism) [Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union).** It was not a single battlefield engagement, but a totalizing system of global tension that reshaped [geography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geography), dictated domestic [culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture), and nearly brought [human civilization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civilization) to an end. As [social studies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_studies) educators, you are tasked with making this invisible war visible to a generation born long after its conclusion. To teach the Cold War is to teach your students how to read the landscape of the modern world—from the political borders of the [Korean peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_Peninsula) to the [national motto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_motto) printed on the [money](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Money) in their pockets. 

## The Architecture of a Divided World

To understand the Cold War, you must first look at a [map](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Map) of a broken [Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe) in [1945](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1945). The geopolitical fault lines were drawn before the smoke of [World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II) had even cleared. **The 1945 [Yalta Conference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yalta_Conference) resulted in an [Allied](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II) agreement to divide postwar [Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Germany) into four separate [occupation zones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allied-occupied_Germany)** ([American](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_occupation_zone_in_Germany), [British](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_occupation_zone_in_Germany), [French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_occupation_zone_in_Germany), and [Soviet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_occupation_zone_in_Germany)). This division was intended to be temporary administrative scaffolding, but it quickly hardened into permanent ideological borders.

![Map showing the division of Germany into American, British, French, and Soviet occupation zones following the 1945 Yalta Conference.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Map-Germany-1945.svg)

By the next year, the ideological friction was palpable. [Winston Churchill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Winston_Churchill) popularized the term "**[Iron Curtain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron_Curtain)**" in a [1946 speech](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinews_of_Peace) to describe the ideological and physical boundary dividing Europe—separating the [democratic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democracy), capitalist West from the [authoritarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Authoritarianism), communist East.

> **[Containment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Containment):** The United States [foreign policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_policy_of_the_United_States) of containment aimed to prevent the spread of communism beyond territories where communism was already established. 

Containment became the central operating system of American foreign policy. It operated on the premise that communism was a [contagion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contagion); you might not cure the infected areas, but you could quarantine them. This policy manifested through massive financial and military commitments:
*   **The [Truman Doctrine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Truman_Doctrine) of [1947](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1947)** pledged United States military and economic assistance to nations threatened by communist insurrection (specifically [Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greece) and [Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey)). 
*   **The [Marshall Plan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marshall_Plan)** provided over \$13 billion in United States economic aid to help rebuild [Western European](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Europe) economies after World War II, under the correct assumption that prosperous nations are highly resistant to [communist revolutions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communist_revolution).

![A map illustrating the relative amounts of economic aid provided to Western European nations under the Marshall Plan.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Marshall_Plan.png)

### The Berlin Crisis and the Alliance System

Nowhere was the friction more volatile than in [Berlin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berlin). Geographically, Berlin sat entirely inside the [Soviet occupation zone of Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_occupation_zone_of_Germany), yet the city itself was divided. In a bid to force the West out of the city, **the Soviet Union blocked all [Western Allied](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Allies) land access to [West Berlin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Berlin) in [1948](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1948)**. 

Instead of retreating or risking a ground war, the United States executed a logistical miracle. **The United States and Western allies supplied West Berlin entirely by [air drops](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Airdrop) during the [Berlin Airlift](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berlin_Airlift) from 1948 to [1949](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1949).** Humiliated, the Soviets eventually lifted the blockade, but the event crystallized the need for formal [military alliances](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_alliance).

![American C-47s unloading supplies at Tempelhof Airport during the Berlin Airlift, overcoming the Soviet land blockade.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/C-47s_at_Tempelhof_Airport_Berlin_1948.jpg)

In 1949, **the [North Atlantic Treaty Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Atlantic_Treaty_Organization) (NATO) was established as a mutual military defense alliance among [North American](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_America) and Western European nations.** The geopolitical calculus shifted again a few years later. When [West Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Germany) was integrated into NATO, the East retaliated in kind: **The Soviet Union and [Eastern Bloc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Bloc) countries formed the [Warsaw Pact](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warsaw_Pact) in [1955](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1955).** **The Warsaw Pact was a [collective defense](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collective_defense) treaty created in direct response to the integration of West Germany into NATO.** 

Years later, to staunch the embarrassing hemorrhage of their own citizens fleeing to the prosperous capitalist enclave of West Berlin, **the [East German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Germany) government began construction of the [Berlin Wall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berlin_Wall) in [1961](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1961) to physically prevent citizens from fleeing to West Berlin.** 

![A map of Cold War Europe divided by the Iron Curtain, separating NATO-aligned Western countries from the Soviet-led Warsaw Pact in the East.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Iron_Curtain_map.svg)

## The Rules of Escalation

The stakes of this rivalry changed forever in 1949. That year, **the Soviet Union successfully detonated the [first Soviet atomic bomb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RDS-1)**. The American monopoly on [nuclear power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_power) was over.

![The mushroom cloud from the first Soviet atomic bomb test (RDS-1) in 1949, which ended the American nuclear monopoly.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Joe_one.jpg)

This initiated a grim new reality in [military science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_science). Both nations adopted the doctrine of **[Mutually Assured Destruction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mutual_assured_destruction) (MAD)**, a [military doctrine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_doctrine) wherein the full-scale use of [nuclear weapons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_weapon) would result in the complete annihilation of both the attacker and the defender. Because direct warfare meant [suicide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suicide), the [superpowers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superpower) utilized **[Brinkmanship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brinkmanship)**, a diplomatic strategy involving the deliberate escalation of dangerous conflicts to the verge of war to force the opposition to concede. 

Because the US and the USSR could not fight each other directly, they fought through surrogates. **[Proxy wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proxy_war) are armed conflicts in which rival superpowers support opposing third parties to fight each other indirectly.**

## The Asian Proxy Battlegrounds

In your classrooms, teaching the map of [Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia) during this era requires explaining the **[Domino Theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domino_theory)**. This theory posited that a communist government takeover in one nation would inevitably lead to communist takeovers in neighboring nations. This fear seemed entirely justified in Washington when **the [Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Communist_Party) under [Mao Zedong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao_Zedong) defeated [Nationalist forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Revolutionary_Army) to establish the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China) in 1949.** 

![A visual representation of the Domino Theory, which posited that if one nation fell to communism, neighboring countries would follow.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Domino_theory.svg)

With the largest population on Earth now under communist rule, the United States drew a hard line on the Korean peninsula.

### The Korean War (1950–1953)
**The [Korean War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_War) began in [1950](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1950) when [North Korean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Korea) communist forces invaded [South Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Korea) by crossing the [38th parallel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/38th_parallel_north).** The [UN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations), led by the United States, intervened to push back the North Koreans. 

The conflict demonstrated the limits of executive power and the dangers of military overreach. **President [Harry S. Truman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_S._Truman) [relieved General Douglas MacArthur of command](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relief_of_General_Douglas_MacArthur) in [1951](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1951) for publicly contradicting United States military policy in [Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korea)**—a vital lesson in [civilian control of the military](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civilian_control_of_the_military). After years of brutal stalemate, **the Korean War ended in a [1953 armistice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_Armistice_Agreement).** The borders barely moved; **the 1953 armistice ending the Korean War established a [Demilitarized Zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_Demilitarized_Zone) near the 38th parallel**, exactly where the conflict had begun.

![An animated map showing the shifting front lines of the Korean War between 1950 and 1953, culminating in a stalemate near the 38th parallel.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Korean_war_1950-1953.gif)

### The Vietnam War (1964–1975)
The Domino Theory drove the United States into its most socially corrosive proxy conflict: Vietnam. Direct American escalation began in earnest when **the [United States Congress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Congress) passed the [Gulf of Tonkin Resolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Tonkin_Resolution) in [1964](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1964)**, which **authorized President [Lyndon B. Johnson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lyndon_B._Johnson) to use direct military force in [Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Asia) without a formal [declaration of war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Declaration_of_war).**

For years, the American public was told victory was imminent. That illusion shattered during **the [Tet Offensive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tet_Offensive) of [1968](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1968), a massive coordinated surprise attack by the [Viet Cong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viet_Cong) and [North Vietnamese forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam_People%27s_Army) against [South Vietnamese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Vietnam) targets.** Though technically a tactical failure for the communist forces, **the heavy casualties of the Tet Offensive caused a severe decline in United States domestic public support for the [Vietnam War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam_War).** 

![Map detailing the major targets of the 1968 Tet Offensive, a massive coordinated surprise attack by communist forces against South Vietnam.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/TetMap.jpg)

The conflict fractured American society, ultimately leading to withdrawal. **The [Paris Peace Accords](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris_Peace_Accords) of [1973](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1973) officially ended direct United States military combat involvement in the Vietnam War.** Without American support, the South collapsed. **[South Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Vietnam) unconditionally surrendered to North Vietnamese forces following the [Fall of Saigon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_of_Saigon) in [1975](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1975).**

## The Crucible: Cuba and the Brink of Annihilation

While conflicts raged in Asia, the Cold War arrived roughly 90 miles off the coast of [Florida](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florida). In [1959](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1959), **[Fidel Castro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fidel_Castro) successfully overthrew the United States-backed [Cuban government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Cuba_%281902%E2%80%931959%29)** and his **[revolutionary government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuban_Revolution) in [Cuba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuba) formally aligned politically and economically with the Soviet Union.**

The United States responded with disastrous incompetence. **The [1961](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1961) [Bay of Pigs Invasion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay_of_Pigs_Invasion) was a failed military attempt by [CIA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency)-sponsored [Cuban exiles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuban_exile) to overthrow Fidel Castro.** This embarrassing failure pushed Castro further into Soviet arms, setting the stage for the most dangerous moment in human history.

**The [Cuban Missile Crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuban_Missile_Crisis) began in [1962](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1962) after United States [reconnaissance photographs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aerial_reconnaissance) revealed Soviet nuclear missile sites under construction in Cuba.** 

![A 1962 aerial reconnaissance photograph taken by a U.S. spy plane revealing Soviet nuclear missile sites under construction in Cuba.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/U2_Image_of_Cuban_Missile_Crisis.jpg)

Rather than a preemptive strike, **the United States instituted a strict [naval blockade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blockade) of Cuba in 1962 to intercept Soviet weapons shipments.** For 13 days, the world stood at the precipice of Mutually Assured Destruction. Brinkmanship yielded a compromise: **The Soviet Union agreed to remove Soviet nuclear missiles from Cuba to resolve the 1962 Cuban Missile Crisis**, provided that the US publicly pledge never to invade Cuba. Behind closed doors, **the United States secretly agreed to remove American [Jupiter nuclear missiles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PGM-19_Jupiter) from [Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey) as part of the resolution to the Cuban Missile Crisis.**

## The Orbiting Battlefield and the Classroom

The Cold War was not confined to terrestrial borders; it extended into the [exosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exosphere) and the American classroom. In [1957](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1957), **the Soviet Union launched [Sputnik 1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sputnik_1), the first artificial [Earth satellite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satellite), into [orbit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orbit).** 

![A replica of Sputnik 1, the first artificial Earth satellite launched by the Soviet Union in 1957, which triggered the Space Race.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Sputnik_asm.jpg)

**The successful Soviet launch of Sputnik 1 initiated the [Space Race](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_Race) between the United States and the Soviet Union.** It also triggered a profound domestic crisis in the US. If the Soviets could put a beeping metal sphere in orbit, they could theoretically drop a nuclear payload anywhere in America. The response fundamentally changed American education: **The United States Congress passed the [National Defense Education Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Defense_Education_Act) in [1958](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1958) to rapidly increase federal funding for [science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science_education) and [math education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics_education).** 

Intelligence gathering evolved as well. High-altitude [espionage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Espionage) became routine until **the [Soviet Air Defence Forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Air_Defence_Forces) shot down a United States [U-2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lockheed_U-2) high-altitude spy plane in [1960](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1960_U-2_incident)**, plunging US-Soviet relations back into a deep freeze. Ultimately, the United States achieved the crowning [propaganda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propaganda) and technological victory of the Space Race when **the United States [Apollo 11](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apollo_11) mission successfully landed the first humans on the [Moon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moon) in [1969](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1969).**

![Astronauts Buzz Aldrin and Neil Armstrong (seen in visor reflection) on the lunar surface during the 1969 Apollo 11 mission.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/A_Man_on_the_Moon%2C_AS11-40-5903_(cropped).jpg)

## The Enemy Within: Paranoia on the Homefront

When teaching [behavioral science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Behavioral_science) and [civics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civics), the domestic impact of the Cold War serves as a masterclass in [mass hysteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass_hysteria) and [civil liberties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civil_liberties). The fear of nuclear annihilation spawned a potent domestic [paranoia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paranoia). 

> **[McCarthyism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McCarthyism):** The practice of making severe allegations of political [subversion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subversion) or [treason](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treason) without proper regard for evidence.

This era of suspicion had roots in genuine espionage. **Former United States [State Department](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Department_of_State) official [Alger Hiss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alger_Hiss) was convicted of [perjury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perjury) in [1950](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1950) regarding his involvement with a Soviet espionage ring.** Even more shockingly, **[Julius and Ethel Rosenberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julius_and_Ethel_Rosenberg) were executed by the United States government in [1953](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1953) for committing espionage by transmitting atomic secrets to the Soviet Union.** 

These legitimate breaches of security were weaponized by politicians to ignite a domestic witch hunt. Congress took the lead: **The [House Un-American Activities Committee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_Un-American_Activities_Committee) (HUAC) investigated alleged communist infiltration within the United States [motion picture industry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cinema_of_the_United_States).** This resulted in the blacklisting of the "**[Hollywood Ten](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hollywood_Ten)**", **a group of [screenwriters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Screenwriter) and [directors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Film_director) cited for [contempt of Congress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contempt_of_Congress) in [1947](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1947) for refusing to testify before the House Un-American Activities Committee.**

The paranoia peaked in the Senate. **Senator [Joseph McCarthy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_McCarthy) led a highly publicized series of aggressive investigations into alleged communist infiltration of the United States government during the early 1950s.** His reign of terror only collapsed when he targeted the US Army. **The televised [Army-McCarthy hearings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Army%E2%80%93McCarthy_hearings) of [1954](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1954) exposed Senator Joseph McCarthy's abusive interrogation tactics to the public.** His power broken, **the [United States Senate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Senate) formally [censured](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Censure_in_the_United_States) Senator Joseph McCarthy in 1954.**

![Chief counsel Joseph N. Welch (left) confronts Senator Joseph McCarthy during the televised Army-McCarthy hearings of 1954, which exposed McCarthy's aggressive tactics.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Welch-McCarthy-Hearings.jpg)

This culture of fear ruined countless lives beyond alleged political dissidents. **The "[Lavender Scare](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lavender_scare)" was a systemic United States government campaign during the 1950s that targeted and terminated [homosexual](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homosexuality) federal employees due to unfounded fears of [blackmail](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blackmail) by Soviet spies.**

### The Militarization of Everyday Life

The psychological toll of the Cold War on children and civic culture was profound. 
*   **The [Federal Civil Defense Administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_Civil_Defense_Administration) encouraged American schoolchildren to practice "[duck and cover](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duck_and_cover)" drills to prepare for potential nuclear bomb detonations.** 
*   To frame the ideological battle as a moral one, **the United States Congress added the phrase "[under God](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Under_God)" to the [Pledge of Allegiance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pledge_of_Allegiance) in 1954 to rhetorically distinguish American society from state-sponsored Soviet [atheism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_atheism).**
*   Similarly, **the United States Congress adopted "[In God We Trust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In_God_We_Trust)" as the official national motto in [1956](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1956).**
*   Even civic infrastructure was militarized. **The United States [Interstate Highway Act of 1956](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal-Aid_Highway_Act_of_1956) was partially justified by the military necessity to rapidly transport troops and evacuate cities during a nuclear crisis.** 

![Schoolchildren practicing a \"duck and cover\" drill in 1962, a civil defense measure to prepare for potential nuclear attacks.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/P.S._58_-_Carroll_%26_Smith_Sts._Bklyn._hold_a_take_cover_drill_01489v.jpg)

## Deflating the Tension: The Era of Détente

After decades of Brinkmanship, the superpowers recognized the unsustainable economic and psychological cost of the standoff. 

> **[Détente](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D%C3%A9tente):** Refers to the deliberate easing of strained geopolitical relations between the United States and the Soviet Union during the 1970s.

A master of [realpolitik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Realpolitik), **President [Richard Nixon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Nixon) visited the communist [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China) in [1972](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1972) to normalize diplomatic relations**, brilliantly exploiting a [diplomatic rift between the Soviet Union and China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sino-Soviet_split). This pressure helped bring the Soviets to the negotiating table. That same year, **the United States and the Soviet Union signed the [Strategic Arms Limitation Talks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strategic_Arms_Limitation_Talks) (SALT I) agreement in 1972 to restrict the number of [intercontinental ballistic missiles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intercontinental_ballistic_missile).** 

![U.S. President Richard Nixon meeting with Chinese Communist Party Chairman Mao Zedong in 1972 to normalize diplomatic relations.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/President_Richard_Nixon_and_Mao_Zedong.jpg)

***

By bridging geopolitics, [economics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economics), and domestic culture, the Cold War provides an unparalleled framework for your future students. It demonstrates how a struggle between two distant capitals can build walls in Germany, shoot satellites into orbit, alter the [curriculum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curriculum) taught in an American classroom, and shape the [highways](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Highway) we still drive on today.
