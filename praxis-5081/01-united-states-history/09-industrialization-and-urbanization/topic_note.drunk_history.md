Why were they flooding through the doors? Push and pull factors! The push is running away from a violent bully: religious persecution in Eastern Europe served as a major push factor for Jewish immigration to the United States, as massive pogroms sent families fleeing for their lives. The pull is the shiny brochure promising a better life: the promise of industrial employment served as a primary pull factor for rural Europeans migrating to America. When they arrived, exhausted and overwhelmed by this massive, chaotic school, they did what any new kid does—they sat with people who understood them.
