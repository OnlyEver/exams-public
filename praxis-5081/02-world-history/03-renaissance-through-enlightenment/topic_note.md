If you want to isolate the exact moment the modern world was born, do not look at a battlefield or a throne room. Look at a painting, and notice how the lines converge. For centuries, [medieval European art](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medieval_art) depicted a flat, hierarchical world where size denoted spiritual importance, not physical reality. But in the 14th century, a mathematical and cultural awakening occurred. Artists realized that by using intersecting lines and a [vanishing point](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanishing_point), they could perfectly map physical reality onto a flat surface. They learned to see the world from the [perspective](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perspective_%28graphical%29) of the human eye. 

![Diagram illustrating linear perspective, where light rays from an object travel to the viewer's eye, creating converging lines that map 3D space onto a 2D surface.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Perspectiva-2.svg)

This single shift—placing the human perspective at the center of reality—is the engine that drives the next four hundred years of [European history](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Europe). As an educator preparing to teach these interconnected eras, you are not merely memorizing a timeline of isolated dates. You are tracing a chain reaction. A shift in art and literature (the [Renaissance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renaissance)) fractures religious unity (the [Reformation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reformation)), forcing decades of bloody conflict that ultimately gives birth to the modern [nation-state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nation-state). Simultaneously, new ways of observing the physical universe (the [Scientific Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_Revolution)) provide the intellectual blueprint for redesigning human government and economics (the [Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment)).

## The Renaissance: Reprogramming the Human Perspective

**The [Renaissance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renaissance) began in the [Italian city-states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_city-states) during the 14th century.** Flourishing trade hubs like [Florence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Florence) and [Venice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Venice) accumulated immense wealth, funding a cultural revival that looked backward to move forward. The intellectual engine of this era was **[Renaissance humanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renaissance_humanism)**, an intellectual movement that **emphasized the study of classical [Greek and Roman literature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_antiquity)**. 

Instead of viewing life merely as a miserable waiting room for the afterlife, **[Renaissance humanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renaissance_humanism) focused on human potential and earthly achievements**. We see this vividly in the art of the period, where **[Renaissance artists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renaissance_art) used [linear perspective](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perspective_%28graphical%29) to create the illusion of [three-dimensional space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three-dimensional_space)**, grounding divine subjects in realistic, mathematical, and decidedly human environments.

Meanwhile, [political philosophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_philosophy) was violently stripped of religious idealism. In 1532, **[Niccolo Machiavelli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niccol%C3%B2_Machiavelli) published the political treatise *[The Prince](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Prince)***. Drawing from his brutal experiences in [Florentine politics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Florence), **[Machiavelli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niccol%C3%B2_Machiavelli) argued in *[The Prince](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Prince)* that political leaders should prioritize maintaining power over acting morally**. This pragmatic, [secular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secularism) view of power is a hallmark of Renaissance thought: observing the world as it *is*, not as it *ought* to be.

### The Hardware of the Renaissance: The Printing Press
If [humanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renaissance_humanism) was the new software of European thought, it required a new hardware distribution system. **[Johannes Gutenberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johannes_Gutenberg) invented the [movable-type printing press](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Printing_press) in Europe around 1440.** 

For a [social studies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_studies) teacher, this is the ultimate "force multiplier" concept. **The European [printing press](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_spread_of_the_printing_press) facilitated the rapid geographic spread of new ideas** and dramatically lowered the cost of books. As a direct result, **the European [printing press](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Printing_press) increased [literacy rates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Literacy) among the [middle class](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_class)**. Without the printing press, the subsequent revolutions in religion and science would have remained localized, easily suppressed controversies.

![Graph showing the exponential increase in European printed book output between the 15th and 18th centuries, demonstrating the printing press's role as a massive force multiplier for new ideas.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/European_Output_of_Printed_Books_ca._1450%E2%80%931800.png)

## The Reformation: Shattering the Universal Church

As [humanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renaissance_humanism) migrated north across the [Alps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alps), it evolved. **[Northern Renaissance humanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_humanism) integrated classical learning with early [Christian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity) ideals.** Unlike the deeply secular Italians, **[Northern Renaissance humanists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Renaissance) sought to reform the practices of the [Catholic Church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Church)**, prioritizing inner piety over external ritual.

This desire for reform reached critical mass in [Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Germany). **[Martin Luther](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martin_Luther) published the [Ninety-five Theses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ninety-five_Theses) in 1517.** Utilizing the [printing press](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Printing_press), Luther's devastating critique of the Church went viral. **The [Protestant Reformation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reformation) challenged the [Catholic Church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Church) practice of selling [indulgences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indulgence)** (certificates purportedly reducing punishment for sins). At the core of his [theology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theology), **[Martin Luther](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martin_Luther) argued that Christian [salvation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salvation_in_Christianity) is achieved through [faith alone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sola_fide)**, rather than through [good works](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Good_works) or church mediation. By asserting that the [Bible](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bible) was the sole source of religious truth, **the [Protestant Reformation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reformation) rejected the ultimate religious authority of the [Pope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pope)**.

![A 16th-century woodcut depicting the Catholic Church's controversial sale of indulgences, the practice that directly provoked Martin Luther's Protestant Reformation.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Jeorg_Breu_Elder_A_Question_to_a_Mintmaker_c1500.png)

Other reformers pushed Luther’s theology further. **[John Calvin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Calvin) published *[Institutes of the Christian Religion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institutes_of_the_Christian_Religion)* in 1536.** The cornerstone of his strict theology was **[Calvinism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calvinism) centered on the theological doctrine of [predestination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Predestination_in_Calvinism).** 

> **[Predestination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Predestination)** is the theological belief that [God](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/God_in_Christianity) has already chosen who will receive [salvation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salvation_in_Christianity), regardless of their actions on Earth.

In [England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/England), the break from [Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rome) was political rather than strictly theological. **[King Henry VIII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_VIII) initiated the [English Reformation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_Reformation) to annul his marriage to [Catherine of Aragon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_of_Aragon)**, who had not produced a male heir. When the [Pope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pope) refused, Henry forced [Parliament](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliament_of_England)'s hand. **The [Act of Supremacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acts_of_Supremacy) in 1534 established the English monarch as the supreme head of the [Church of England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Church_of_England)**, permanently intertwining English national identity with [Protestantism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protestantism).

### The Catholic Counter-Reformation
The [Catholic Church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Church) did not capitulate. To combat the Protestant threat, **the Catholic Church convened the [Council of Trent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Council_of_Trent) between 1545 and 1563**. 
* Doctrinally, **the [Council of Trent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Council_of_Trent) reaffirmed traditional Catholic [theological doctrines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_theology)** (such as the necessity of both faith and [good works](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Good_works), and the authority of the [Pope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pope)). 
* Administratively, **the [Council of Trent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Council_of_Trent) instituted internal reforms to address Catholic [clerical corruption](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anticlericalism)**, strictly forbidding the sale of [indulgences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indulgence).

To enforce these reforms, **[Ignatius of Loyola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ignatius_of_Loyola) founded the [Society of Jesus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Society_of_Jesus) in 1540.** Known as the [Jesuits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Society_of_Jesus), **the [Society of Jesus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Society_of_Jesus) focused on education and missionary work to counter [Protestantism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protestantism)**, deploying highly educated priests across Europe and the newly discovered Americas.

## Blood and Borders: The Wars of Religion

The shattering of religious unity plunged [Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe) into a [century of bloodshed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_wars_of_religion). States struggled to answer a sudden, terrifying question: *How can a nation function if its citizens do not share the same religion?*

The early compromises were brittle. **The [Peace of Augsburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peace_of_Augsburg) in 1555 allowed German princes to choose the religion of their respective territories**, creating a patchwork of Catholic and Protestant states in the [Holy Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire). In [France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France), after decades of sectarian slaughter, **[King Henry IV of France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_IV_of_France) issued the [Edict of Nantes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edict_of_Nantes) in 1598**. **The [Edict of Nantes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edict_of_Nantes) granted religious rights and protections to French Protestant [Huguenots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huguenots)**, an early, pragmatic attempt at [religious pluralism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religious_pluralism).

However, the ultimate explosion occurred in the 17th century. **The [Thirty Years War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War) lasted from 1618 to 1648**, devastating central Europe. When the exhaustion finally brought the powers to the negotiating table, **the [Peace of Westphalia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peace_of_Westphalia) in 1648 ended the [Thirty Years War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War).** 

> **Why [Westphalia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peace_of_Westphalia) Matters for [Civics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civics) and [Geography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geography):**
> **The [Peace of Westphalia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peace_of_Westphalia) established the modern diplomatic principle of [state sovereignty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westphalian_system).** It decreed that rulers have exclusive authority within their own borders, free from external religious or political interference. When you look at a modern map with strictly defined borders, you are looking at the legacy of [Westphalia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westphalian_system).

![Map of Europe following the 1648 Peace of Westphalia, which established the modern geographic and diplomatic framework of strictly defined borders and sovereign nation-states.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Europe_map_1648.PNG)

## The Scientific Revolution: Reading the Mind of God

Simultaneous to the religious wars, a quieter but vastly more profound upheaval was occurring. **The [Scientific Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_Revolution) marked a shift toward using observation and [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics) to understand the natural world**, abandoning reliance on ancient Greek texts and Church dogma.

The catalyst was [astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomy). **[Nicolaus Copernicus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicolaus_Copernicus) proposed the [heliocentric model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliocentrism) of the universe in 1543.** This **[heliocentric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliocentrism) astronomical model placed the [Sun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun) at the center of the [solar system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_System)**, displacing [Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth) from its privileged center. 

![Comparison of the Ptolemaic geocentric model (top) and the Copernican heliocentric model (bottom), which mathematically placed the Sun at the center of the solar system.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Geoz_wb_en.svg)

Later astronomers built upon this. **[Johannes Kepler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johannes_Kepler) formulated the foundational [laws of planetary motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kepler%27s_laws_of_planetary_motion).** Utilizing massive data sets, **[Johannes Kepler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johannes_Kepler) demonstrated mathematically that planets move in [elliptical orbits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elliptic_orbit)**, not the perfect circles demanded by classical philosophy. The deathblow to the old worldview came when **[Galileo Galilei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galileo_Galilei) used a [telescope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telescope) to discover the [largest moons of Jupiter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galilean_moons)**. Seeing celestial bodies orbiting something *other* than Earth meant **[Galileo Galilei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galileo_Galilei) made astronomical observations that directly contradicted the [geocentric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geocentric_model) teachings of the [Catholic Church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Church)**, earning him a trial by the [Inquisition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Inquisition).

![Diagram of Kepler's first law of planetary motion, illustrating that planets travel in an elliptical orbit around the Sun, breaking from the classical belief in perfect celestial circles.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Kepler-first-law.svg)

The revolution culminated with an English physicist. **[Isaac Newton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isaac_Newton) published *[Principia Mathematica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophi%C3%A6_Naturalis_Principia_Mathematica)* in 1687.** In it, **[Isaac Newton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isaac_Newton) formulated [laws of motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newton%27s_laws_of_motion) that provided a unified mathematical framework for [terrestrial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_mechanics) and [celestial mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celestial_mechanics).** An apple falling from a tree and a planet orbiting the sun were governed by the exact same mathematical laws. 

Underpinning these discoveries were new methodologies:
* **[Francis Bacon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francis_Bacon) developed the [empirical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empiricism) [scientific method](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_method) based on [inductive reasoning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inductive_reasoning)** (gathering specific data points to form a general conclusion).
* **[Rene Descartes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Descartes) emphasized [deductive reasoning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deductive_reasoning) and [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics) in scientific inquiry** (starting from self-evident truths and logically deducing complex realities).

## The Enlightenment: Engineering Society

Here is the cognitive bridge you must help your students cross: *If the universe is a rational, clockwork mechanism governed by predictable [natural laws](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_law) ([Newton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isaac_Newton)), shouldn't human society, government, and [economics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economics) also be governed by rational, natural laws?* 

**The [Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment) applied the rational principles of the [Scientific Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_Revolution) to human society and government.** The resulting political theories provided the vocabulary for the modern [democratic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democracy) world. 

### The Evolution of the Social Contract

| Philosopher | Core Text | Core Philosophy & Concept |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[Thomas Hobbes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Hobbes)** | *[Leviathan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leviathan_%28Hobbes_book%29)* (1651) | Wrote during the bloody [English Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_Civil_War). **[Thomas Hobbes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Hobbes) argued that humans require a strong [absolute monarch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Absolute_monarchy) to prevent the chaos of the [state of nature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_of_nature).** |
| **[John Locke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Locke)** | *[Two Treatises of Government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Two_Treatises_of_Government)* (1689) | **[John Locke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Locke) argued that all individuals possess [natural rights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_rights_and_legal_rights) to life, liberty, and property.** Furthermore, **[John Locke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Locke) theorized that a government loses its legitimacy if it fails to protect the [natural rights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_rights_and_legal_rights) of citizens.** |
| **[Jean-Jacques Rousseau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Jacques_Rousseau)** | *[The Social Contract](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Social_Contract)* (1762) | **[Jean-Jacques Rousseau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Jacques_Rousseau) argued that legitimate political authority comes directly from the [general will](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_will) of the people**, not from monarchs or elites. |

Beyond the [social contract](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_contract), other French *[philosophes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophe)* constructed the structural mechanics of modern freedom:
* **[Montesquieu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montesquieu) published *[The Spirit of the Laws](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Spirit_of_the_Laws)* in 1748.** Observing the danger of absolute power, **[Montesquieu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montesquieu) advocated for the [separation of government powers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Separation_of_powers) into [executive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_%28government%29), [legislative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legislature), and [judicial branches](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judiciary).** Why? Because **the [separation of government powers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Separation_of_powers) prevents any single branch of government from becoming [tyrannical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tyranny).**
* **[Voltaire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voltaire) was a prominent French *[philosophe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophe)* who aggressively advocated for [freedom of speech](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freedom_of_speech).** Furthermore, **[Voltaire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voltaire) strongly defended [religious tolerance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religious_tolerance) against the dogmas of the established church.** 

The collective ambition of this era was staggering. **[Denis Diderot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denis_Diderot) served as the chief editor of the French *[Encyclopedie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Encyclop%C3%A9die)*.** **The *[Encyclopedie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Encyclop%C3%A9die)* aimed to compile and disseminate all [Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment) knowledge across Europe**, effectively democratizing information. The prevailing religious view of the intellectuals was no longer orthodox [Christianity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity), but **[Deism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deism)**. **[Deism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deism) is an [Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment) belief that a divine creator set the universe in motion and allowed it to run according to [natural laws](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_law)**, much like a clockmaker who winds a watch and steps back.

![The "figurative system of human knowledge" from Diderot's Encyclopédie, which ambitiously reorganized all human learning based on memory, reason, and imagination rather than orthodox religious dogma.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/ENC_SYSTEME_FIGURE.jpeg)

### The Natural Laws of Wealth
The [Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment) also revolutionized economics. **[Adam Smith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adam_Smith) published *[The Wealth of Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Wealth_of_Nations)* in 1776.** Prior to Smith, European empires operated on *[mercantilism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mercantilism)*—the belief that global wealth was finite, requiring governments to strictly control trade and hoard gold. 

**[Adam Smith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adam_Smith) proposed [economic theories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economics) that fundamentally opposed the state-controlled [mercantilist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mercantilism) systems of the era.** Instead, **[Adam Smith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adam_Smith) argued for a [free-market economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_market) guided by the [invisible hand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Invisible_hand) of [supply and demand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supply_and_demand).** He posited that individuals pursuing their own rational [self-interest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rational_egoism) naturally generate economic prosperity for the entire society.

![A fundamental free-market supply and demand curve, illustrating Adam Smith's theory that rational self-interest naturally drives economic equilibrium without state-controlled mercantilism.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Supply-demand-equilibrium.svg)

## The Shockwaves: Revolutions and Rebellions

[Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment) theories did not stay locked in Parisian salons; they became the operating instructions for global revolution.

In [North America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_America), **[Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment) ideals directly inspired the [American Declaration of Independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Declaration_of_Independence).** When drafting the document, **[Thomas Jefferson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Jefferson) incorporated [John Locke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Locke)'s concept of [natural rights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_rights_and_legal_rights) into the [American Declaration of Independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Declaration_of_Independence)**—substituting "pursuit of happiness" for "property."

In Europe, **[Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment) philosophies fueled the [French Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Revolution) demands for civic equality and individual liberty.** Tearing down the [absolute monarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Absolute_monarchy), **the [French National Assembly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Assembly_%28French_Revolution%29) issued the [Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Declaration_of_the_Rights_of_Man_and_of_the_Citizen) in 1789**, a document deeply steeped in [Rousseau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Jacques_Rousseau)'s concept of the [general will](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_will) and [Locke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Locke)'s [natural rights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_rights_and_legal_rights).

![The 1789 Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen, a foundational French Revolution document explicitly built upon Enlightenment concepts of natural rights and the general will.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Declaration_of_the_Rights_of_Man_and_of_the_Citizen_in_1789.jpg)

Not all changes required the [guillotine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guillotine). Some monarchs adapted. **[Enlightened absolutism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enlightened_absolutism) involved European monarchs adopting [Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment) ideas to improve their nations without surrendering [sovereign power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereignty).** For example, **[Frederick the Great](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frederick_the_Great) of [Prussia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prussia) implemented religious toleration policies inspired by [Enlightenment philosophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment)**, recognizing that [religious persecution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religious_persecution) was irrational and harmful to the state's economic efficiency.

Finally, the shockwave crossed the Atlantic once more. **[Latin American](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin_America) revolutionaries used [Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment) concepts to justify their [wars of independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_American_wars_of_independence) against [Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Empire).** The great liberator **[Simon Bolivar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sim%C3%B3n_Bol%C3%ADvar) cited [Enlightenment thinkers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment) in his letters advocating for South American political independence**, using [Montesquieu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montesquieu) and [Locke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Locke) to argue against the tyranny of [Spanish colonial rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Empire).

**For the Teacher:**
As you review these concepts for the [Praxis 5081](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Praxis_test) exam, always look for the causal chain. The [Renaissance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renaissance) unlocked human potential; the [printing press](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Printing_press) distributed it; the [Reformation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reformation) challenged traditional authority; the [Scientific Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_Revolution) provided a mathematical framework for truth; and the [Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment) weaponized that truth to build the modern political world. When you understand the "why" that connects these eras, the "what" and the "when" become impossible to forget.
