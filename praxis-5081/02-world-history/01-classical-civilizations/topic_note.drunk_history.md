To keep the patricians from going full tyrant, the Roman Republic government included two elected consuls who held executive power for one-year terms. They could veto each other—a buddy-cop system for statecraft. Backing them up, the Roman Republic Senate was an advisory legislative body composed primarily of wealthy patricians. 

Eventually, the plebeians got sick of the patricians making up the rules on the fly. Imagine a teacher changing the grading rubric after you hand in the test. Nightmare! This is massive because the Twelve Tables established the principle that all free Roman citizens had a right to legal protection. You can't just punish someone because you feel like it! In fact, Roman legal principles such as the presumption of innocence heavily influenced the foundation of modern Western legal systems. 

Then Rome got greedy. They looked across the Mediterranean and saw the Carthaginian Empire with all its lunch money. Following a period of absolute chaos, the Qin Dynasty successfully ended the Warring States period and unified China in 221 BCE. How? By being the most terrifying, unyielding substitute teacher on the planet. The Qin Dynasty strictly adopted Legalism as its sole official state philosophy. Legalism is an ancient Chinese philosophy emphasizing stringent laws and harsh punishments to forcibly maintain social order. It assumes everyone is inherently bad and will only behave if terrified. The emperor, Qin Shi Huangdi, aggressively standardized weights, volumetric measures, and the written script across unified China. No deviations! Everyone uses the exact same pencil!
