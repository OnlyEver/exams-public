Slide that bowl of pretzels over here and listen to me, because the human mind is a wild, unhinged reality TV show filmed inside a mob-run restaurant, and you’re the poor sap who just got hired to direct it. If you want to survive the night shift, you need to understand how the chaos starts. We’re talking about the difference between a grease fire and a rotting foundation. Acute mental illness symptoms manifest rapidly over a short duration—boom, the deep fryer explodes out of nowhere and the kitchen is up in flames! But chronic mental illness symptoms persist continuously for months or years, like a slow-leaking pipe that quietly rots the floorboards under the dining room until the whole thing eventually caves in. 

Before you try to put out the fire, you have to read the room. That’s what the Mental Status Examination is for. The Mental Status Examination assesses a client's current cognitive functioning—how the gears in their head are turning right this second—and the Mental Status Examination assesses a client's current emotional functioning. You have to separate the written script from the actor's performance. Mood is the subjective emotional state directly reported by the client. It’s exactly what they tell you in the reality TV confessional booth. But affect is the objective outward expression of a client's emotional state observed by the clinician. It’s what you actually see on their face when the cameras roll. Sometimes, the face is entirely bankrupt of drama. A flat affect involves the complete clinical absence of emotional expression. They win the million-dollar prize, and they look like a brick wall. A blunted affect is just a notch above that; it involves a severe reduction in the outward intensity of emotional expression. Maybe you get a slight eyebrow twitch for the million bucks. But then you have the explosive divas! A labile affect involves rapid and extreme pathological shifts in emotional expression. They are laughing, then sobbing hysterically, then screaming in a blinding rage all in the span of ten seconds! You also have to check their higher-level thinking on this exam. Judgment refers to a client's cognitive ability to make safe and socially appropriate decisions, like deciding not to throw a hot cocktail in the host's face. Insight refers to a client's psychological awareness of possessing a mental illness. Do they realize their brain is feeding them a bad script, or do they truly believe everyone else is the problem?

Now, let’s talk about the physical hardware failing in the restaurant. Delirium is an acute state of cognitive confusion. It’s a sudden, violent mob hit on the brain! Delirium typically presents with a sudden onset over hours or days, and the dead giveaway is that delirium involves a fluctuating level of consciousness. The guy is wide awake and terrified at noon, and practically comatose by three o'clock. Dementia, on the other hand, is a chronic neurocognitive disorder. It’s not a sudden hit; dementia typically presents with an insidious and gradual onset. The most common physiological cause of dementia is Alzheimer's disease. The memory files are slowly being erased by a rival syndicate, and an early clinical sign of dementia is impaired short-term memory. They can tell you exactly what kind of getaway car they drove in 1962, but they don’t know what they ate for breakfast today. When the recent files go completely missing, the brain panics and tries a desperate cover-up. We see confabulation, which is the unconscious psychological filling of memory gaps with fabricated information. They aren’t lying to you on purpose to be malicious; their brain is just auto-generating a fake backstory so they don’t look weak. Naturally, confabulation is commonly observed in clients diagnosed with severe dementia.

As the mob war in the brain worsens, the trusted henchmen start dropping the ball. Aphasia is the communications guy getting his wires cut; aphasia is the neurological loss of ability to understand speech, or conversely, aphasia is the neurological loss of ability to express speech. The muscle of the operation goes down with apraxia, which is the inability to perform purposeful movements despite entirely intact motor function. His arms work perfectly fine, but his brain completely forgot the recipe for swinging a bat! Then the lookout guy gets compromised with agnosia, which is the neurological inability to recognize familiar objects or people. He stares right at a police car or his own mother, and his brain has zero clue what he’s looking at. Eventually, the mob boss himself gets taken out. Executive dysfunction impairs a client's cognitive ability to plan complex tasks, and executive dysfunction impairs a client's cognitive ability to organize complex tasks. The whole heist falls apart because the boss can't even figure out how to put his socks on, let alone organize the getaway drivers.

But what happens when the reality TV show completely switches genres into a sci-fi horror movie? Acute psychotic episodes involve a sudden psychological break from reality. If this terrifying new script doesn’t get canceled, it becomes a permanent running series. A formal diagnosis of schizophrenia requires the continuous presence of symptoms for at least six months. The producers of this twisted show use two main tricks: they add crazy special effects, and they slash the budget on the normal stuff. Positive symptoms of schizophrenia represent pathological psychological additions to normal baseline behavior. They put things in the episode that shouldn’t be there! Delusions are clinically classified as a positive symptom of schizophrenia. Hallucinations are clinically classified as a positive symptom of schizophrenia. Disorganized speech is clinically classified as a positive symptom of schizophrenia. 

Let’s look at the bad scripts, also known as delusions. Grandiose delusions involve a firmly held exaggerated belief in personal power or importance. "I am the secret emperor of the television network!" Persecutory delusions involve the firmly held false belief that a client is being singled out for harm. "The makeup artist is trying to assassinate me with poisoned powder!" Somatic delusions involve a firmly held false belief regarding a physical defect or medical condition. "My stomach is literally filled with solid concrete!" Erotomanic delusions involve the false belief that another person is romantically in love with the client. "Brad Pitt is secretly obsessed with me and sends me love letters through the wind!"

You better know the difference between bad lighting and a completely fake monster. Illusions are perceptual misinterpretations of actual external physical stimuli. You see a coat hanging on a rack in the dark and think it’s a sniper. But hallucinations are sensory perceptions experienced in the absolute absence of an external physical stimulus. There is no coat rack; you’re just seeing a sniper in an empty, locked, brightly lit room! Auditory hallucinations are the most common sensory type of hallucination in schizophrenia. The absolute worst of these are command hallucinations, which explicitly instruct a client to perform a specific physical action, like demonic voices screaming at them to smash a window.

When the teleprompter shatters, you get disorganized speech. Flight of ideas is characterized by rapid verbal shifting between unrelated topics. "I like cats, look at that car, my shoe is blue, let's eat cheese!" Tangentiality occurs when a client wanders off the conversational topic without ever answering the original question. They get in a cab, drive off a cliff, and never come back to the point. Circumstantiality occurs when a client provides excessive unnecessary detail before eventually answering a question. They take you on a massive detour through their grandmother's entire history just to tell you what time it is, but they do finally answer you. Neologisms are newly coined nonsensical words created by a client. "Pass me the flim-flam-bazzle!" Word salad is a severely disorganized vocal mixture of isolated words lacking any logical meaning. It’s just dictionary words thrown in a blender and poured on the floor. Clang associations involve the verbal grouping of words based on rhyming sounds rather than logical meaning. "The boat, the goat, I wear a coat, don't gloat!" Sometimes they just turn into a biological parrot. Echolalia is the pathological vocal repeating of another person's words. And while it’s not speech, echopraxia is the pathological physical imitation of another person's movements. You cross your arms, they cross their arms.

Now for the network budget cuts. Negative symptoms of schizophrenia represent pathological psychological deficits in normal baseline behavior. The network takes away all the good stuff. Anhedonia is the psychological inability to experience pleasure from normally enjoyable activities. Winning the show feels like eating dust. Avolition is a pathological lack of motivation to initiate goal-directed activities. The starter motor is broken, and they won’t even get off the couch to cash the prize check. Alogia is a pathological poverty of speech. They just stop talking entirely. The microphone is dead.

The ratings for this show are a rollercoaster of agony. Major depressive disorder is clinically characterized by a persistent depressed mood. To get that miserable diagnosis, a diagnosis of major depressive disorder requires mood symptoms to last for a minimum of two consecutive weeks. And you better be watching them like a hawk, because suicidal ideation is a prominent life-threatening symptom of major depressive disorder. They want to cancel their own show permanently. But then, the ratings skyrocket into a dangerous, electric frenzy! Bipolar I disorder requires a documented clinical history of at least one manic episode. A manic episode is clinically characterized by an abnormally elevated or irritable mood, and a manic episode requires clinical mood symptoms to last for at least one consecutive week. How do you spot the frenzy? A markedly decreased physiological need for sleep is a hallmark symptom of a manic episode. They are remodeling the entire kitchen at three in the morning for five days straight on zero sleep, fueled purely by chaos!

Sometimes the stress in the kitchen is just too much. Generalized anxiety disorder involves excessive psychological worrying lasting for at least six consecutive months. Half a year of agonizing over whether the soup is going to burn! But a panic attack is a completely different beast. Panic attacks are abrupt physiological surges of intense fear. It hits like a freight train out of nowhere, and the intense fear of a panic attack reaches a peak physiological intensity within minutes. To cope with the chaos, some people develop desperate rituals. Obsessions are recurrent and persistent intrusive psychological thoughts. "Are my hands covered in lethal germs? Are they?!" Compulsions are repetitive physical or mental behaviors performed to reduce anxiety associated with an obsession. They scrub their hands fifty times in boiling water just to make their brain stop screaming. 

If the kitchen literally explodes, we get into severe trauma. Post-traumatic stress disorder develops following psychological exposure to a traumatic event. But dropping a plate doesn’t count. A qualifying trauma in post-traumatic stress disorder involves actual or threatened death or serious injury. Their brain creates horrific instant replays; flashbacks are a severe dissociative symptom of post-traumatic stress disorder. Because the memories are so agonizing, avoidance of trauma-related stimuli is a core diagnostic symptom of post-traumatic stress disorder. They will refuse to ever walk past a kitchen or smell smoke again.

When the world feels completely out of control, people try to desperately control their own bodies. Anorexia nervosa is an eating disorder characterized by a morbid psychological fear of obesity. To fight this terrifying fear, clients with anorexia nervosa exhibit severe physical restriction of food intake. They put a massive padlock on their own pantry. Bulimia nervosa is characterized by recurrent episodic periods of excessive binge eating. They lose control and eat the entire buffet, but the crushing guilt takes over, and bulimia nervosa involves compensatory purging behaviors following a binge eating episode. They try to violently hit the "undo" button through vomiting or punishing exercise.

Do you know how the ego survives all this unbearable drama? It hires a sleazy PR team to spin the truth. These are the defense mechanisms. Denial is a defense mechanism involving the psychological refusal to accept objective reality. "The kitchen isn't on fire, it's just a warm new lighting choice!" Projection is a defense mechanism attributing unacceptable personal feelings to another external person. You're secretly furious, so you scream, "Why are you so mad at me?!" Rationalization is a defense mechanism using logical explanations to justify unacceptable psychological behaviors. "I only punched the producer because he breathed too loud." Displacement is a defense mechanism redirecting emotions from a threatening target to a safer alternative target. The network boss yells at you, so you go home and kick the garbage can. Reaction formation is a defense mechanism expressing an outward attitude opposite to an unacceptable unconscious feeling. You absolutely despise your co-star, so you act sickly sweet and buy them flowers every single day. Regression is a defense mechanism involving a return to an earlier developmental psychological stage under severe stress. The ratings drop, so the forty-year-old lead actor curls up in a ball and sucks his thumb! Splitting is a defense mechanism psychologically characterizing people or situations as entirely good or entirely bad. You are either an absolute angel of mercy or a literal demon; there is no gray area.

Which brings us to the uncastable divas—the personality disorders. These are grouped into three distinct behavioral clusters. Cluster A personality disorders are characterized by odd or eccentric interpersonal behaviors. That’s the guy who lives in the prop closet hoarding tin foil. Cluster B personality disorders are characterized by dramatic or erratic interpersonal behaviors. These are your screaming table-flippers! Cluster C personality disorders are characterized by anxious or fearful interpersonal behaviors. They are terrified of their own shadow. Let's look at the absolute nightmares in Cluster B. Antisocial personality disorder involves a pervasive lifelong disregard for the fundamental rights of others. Clients with antisocial personality disorder routinely exhibit a clinical lack of remorse for destructive personal actions. They will steal your car, crash it into a tree, and sleep like a baby. Borderline personality disorder involves severe emotional instability in interpersonal relationships, and borderline personality disorder is characterized by extreme behavioral impulsivity. They will marry you on Tuesday and set your clothes on fire on Wednesday! Remember that PR spin tactic we just talked about? Splitting is a hallmark clinical symptom of borderline personality disorder. They idolize you as their savior in the morning, then they completely despise you as a monster by nightfall. Narcissistic personality disorder involves a pervasive psychological pattern of grandiosity. Narcissistic personality disorder involves a profound psychological lack of empathy for others. They think they are the literal center of the universe, and they do not care who they crush to stay in the spotlight.

So, how do we fix this catastrophic mess of a show? We bring in the specialized fixers. Cognitive behavioral therapy aims to identify distorted psychological thought patterns, and then cognitive behavioral therapy aims to alter distorted psychological thought patterns. You find the rotten script, cross it out, and write a better one! Dialectical behavior therapy heavily focuses on improving psychological emotional regulation, and dialectical behavior therapy heavily focuses on building psychological distress tolerance. We teach the borderline diva how to tolerate feeling upset without throwing a chair through a window. When the star is so depressed they won't even get out of bed and the meds do absolutely nothing, you have to reboot the power grid. Electroconvulsive therapy is utilized to treat severe clinical depression unresponsive to pharmacological medication. Sometimes, the only way to heal is to completely change the set. Milieu therapy uses a safe and structured physical environment to facilitate psychological patient recovery. The walls, the daily routine, the calm lighting—everything in the room becomes the medicine. 

Finally, as the nurse directing all of this, you have to watch out for your own mental traps! Transference occurs when a client unconsciously projects feelings about past figures onto a healthcare provider. The patient screams at you because your haircut reminds them of their terrible ex-husband! But watch yourself, because countertransference occurs when a healthcare provider unconsciously projects personal feelings onto a client. You give a patient extra dessert just because they remind you of your sweet old grandpa. It's a madhouse in there, my friend, but if you can read the scripts, spot the bad actors, and keep the kitchen from burning down, you just might survive the season! Now, are you going to order another round or what?
