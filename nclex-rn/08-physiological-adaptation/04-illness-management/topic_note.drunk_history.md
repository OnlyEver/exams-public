Okay, let's talk about the wildest mechanical failure: breathing. People confuse the bellows with the payload! Ventilation is just the bellows pumping. Impaired ventilation involves a disruption in the mechanical movement of air into the lungs. Oxygenation is completely different! That’s the payload crossing the border. Impaired oxygenation occurs upon inadequate transfer of oxygen from the alveoli into the pulmonary capillaries. We track these with rigid, unforgiving numbers. For the payload, normal arterial oxygen saturation ranges from 95 percent to 100 percent. If it tanks, panic ensues, because an arterial oxygen saturation below 90 percent is a clinical indicator of hypoxemia. For ventilation, we look at the exhaust—the carbon dioxide. Normal partial pressure of arterial carbon dioxide is between 35 and 45 millimeters of mercury. If you stop moving air, the exhaust backs up and turns the blood into battery acid! A partial pressure of arterial carbon dioxide greater than 45 millimeters of mercury indicates respiratory acidosis.
