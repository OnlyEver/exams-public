First, assessing a client's ability to manage care at home requires evaluating their capacity to perform Activities of Daily Living, or ADLs. To measure this, we use the Katz Index of Independence in Activities of Daily Living. Think of the Katz Index as the street ledger; it's an assessment tool that measures a client's ability to perform basic self-care tasks independently.

Assessing a client's ability to manage care at home requires evaluating their capacity to perform Instrumental Activities of Daily Living, or IADLs. Instrumental Activities of Daily Living include complex tasks such as managing finances, handling transportation, shopping, preparing meals, and managing medications. We audit this using the Lawton Instrumental Activities of Daily Living Scale, which assesses a client's independent living skills.
