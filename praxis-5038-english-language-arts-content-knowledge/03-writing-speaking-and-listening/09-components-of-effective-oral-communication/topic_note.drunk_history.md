Now, you can't just let them loose as a massive, unstructured mob. You are running a crew, and every heist needs a specialized team. Assigning specific roles to students ensures individual accountability in collaborative discussions. Nobody gets to just ride in the trunk while the rest of the group does the intellectual labor! Common collaborative discussion roles include facilitator, recorder, timekeeper, and summarizer. The facilitator in a group discussion guides the conversation to keep participants on topic—they are your getaway driver steering the car out of tangent-alley. But my absolute favorite role? The mole. The devil's advocate discussion role requires a student to intentionally challenge the group's consensus.
