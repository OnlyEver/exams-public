Want a bar trick? To plot a line using the x-intercept and y-intercept, you just graph the two intercept points on the axes and draw a straight line through them! Boom. Alliance exposed. 

Now, every alliance has a trajectory, a vibe, a momentum. In math, we call this the slope. The slope of a line measures the steepness and the direction of that line. It tells you exactly who is climbing the social ladder and who is getting dragged. Slope is calculated as the ratio of the vertical change (the rise) to the horizontal change (the run) between any two points on a given line. If you have two gossiping points, you track the drama with a formula. The formula for the slope between two points (x1, y1) and (x2, y2) is m = (y2 - y1) / (x2 - x1). You're literally taking the difference in their elevator floors and dividing it by the distance down the hallway!
