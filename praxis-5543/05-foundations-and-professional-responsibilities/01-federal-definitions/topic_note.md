When an [engineer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Engineer) builds a [bridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bridge), the first step is not pouring [concrete](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concrete); it is defining the precise [tolerances](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Engineering_tolerance) of the [materials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Material). Without a shared [vocabulary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vocabulary) of exact [specifications](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Specification_%28technical_standard%29), the [structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Structure) inevitably fails. In the realm of [education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Education), the **[Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Individuals_with_Disabilities_Education_Act)** serves as our [structural engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Structural_engineering) code. The [Individuals with Disabilities Education Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Individuals_with_Disabilities_Education_Act) is the [federal law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_law_of_the_United_States) that governs how states and public agencies provide [early intervention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Early_childhood_intervention), [special education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education), and related services. 

For an [educator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teacher), these federal definitions are not abstract [bureaucratic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bureaucracy) hurdles. They are the strict legal thresholds that dictate whether a child receives a lifeline of support or falls through the cracks of the [general education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainstreaming_%28education%29) system. A [medical diagnosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medical_diagnosis) alone does not grant a student a seat in your [special education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education) classroom; it is the federal criteria that translate observable student struggles into legally protected educational rights. Understanding these classifications is how you navigate the machinery of the school system to [advocate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advocacy) for the students sitting in front of you.

## The Architecture of IDEA: Parts, Prongs, and Services

Before we can categorize [disabilities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disability), we must understand the machinery that delivers services. IDEA is divided by the [developmental timeline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Child_development) of the child.
*   **Part C** of the [Individuals with Disabilities Education Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Individuals_with_Disabilities_Education_Act) ensures that [early intervention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Early_childhood_intervention) services are provided to eligible [infants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infant) and [toddlers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toddler) from birth through age two. 
*   **Part B** takes over as the child grows, dictating that [special education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education) and related services are provided to eligible children and [youth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Youth) ages three through twenty-one.

### The Two-Pronged Gatekeeper

How does a child actually qualify for these services? The [law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Law) establishes a rigid two-prong test. A "child with a disability" under the [Individuals with Disabilities Education Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Individuals_with_Disabilities_Education_Act) must be [evaluated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Educational_assessment) as:
1.  Having one of the federally defined [disabilities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disability), **AND**
2.  Needing [special education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education) and related services *by reason of that disability*.

> **The Exam Trap:** What if a child has a federally defined [disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disability) (like a [physical impairment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_disability)) but only needs a related service (like [physical therapy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_therapy)) and *not* [special education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education)? In this scenario, the child is generally not considered a "child with a disability" under the [Individuals with Disabilities Education Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Individuals_with_Disabilities_Education_Act). To unlock IDEA, the student must require [specially designed instruction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education).

![The special education evaluation process hinges on the two-pronged test: identifying a disability and proving the educational need for specially designed instruction.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Special_Education_Process.jpg)

### Defining the Support Network

If a student passes the two-prong test, they are entitled to a specific ecosystem of support. We must be fiercely precise about what these terms mean.

Under the [Individuals with Disabilities Education Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Individuals_with_Disabilities_Education_Act), **"[special education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education)"** means specially designed instruction provided at no cost to parents to meet the unique needs of a child with a [disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disability). It is the modification of content, [methodology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teaching_method), or delivery.

To support that instruction, the law mandates two supplementary pillars:
1.  **Related Services:** These are [transportation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Student_transport) and developmental, corrective, or supportive services required to assist a child with a [disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disability) to *benefit* from [special education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education). (Think of [speech-language pathology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speech-language_pathology), [occupational therapy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occupational_therapy), or specialized transport). 
    *   *The Crucial Exception:* Related services under the [Individuals with Disabilities Education Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Individuals_with_Disabilities_Education_Act) do *not* include a [medical device](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medical_device) that is [surgically implanted](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Implant_%28medicine%29), or the replacement of such a device. The school will provide [speech therapy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speech-language_pathology) for a student with a [cochlear implant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cochlear_implant), but they are not legally responsible for replacing the implant's internal hardware.

![Under IDEA's related services exception, schools are not legally or financially responsible for surgically implanted medical devices, such as the internal hardware of a cochlear implant.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Cochearimplants.JPG)

2.  **Supplementary Aids and Services:** These are supports provided in [regular education classes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainstreaming_%28education%29) to enable children with [disabilities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disability) to be educated with nondisabled children to the maximum extent appropriate. (Think of preferential seating, adapted materials, or a 1:1 [paraprofessional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paraprofessional_educator) in a general education [math](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics) class).

## The Four Pillars of Legal Compliance

Every decision you make as a [special educator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education) operates under four foundational mandates. 

### 1. Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE)
The cornerstone of IDEA is that every eligible child receives [FAPE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_Appropriate_Public_Education). But "appropriate" is a [legal term of art](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Term_of_art). "[Free Appropriate Public Education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_Appropriate_Public_Education)" requires [special education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education) and related services to be provided at public expense, under public supervision, and without charge. Furthermore, a [Free Appropriate Public Education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_Appropriate_Public_Education) must:
*   Meet the standards of the [State Educational Agency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_education_agency).
*   Include an appropriate [preschool](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Preschool), [elementary school](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elementary_school), or [secondary school](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secondary_school) education in the state involved.
*   Require [special education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education) services to be provided in conformity with an [Individualized Education Program](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Individualized_Education_Program).

### 2. The Individualized Education Program (IEP)
An **"[Individualized Education Program](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Individualized_Education_Program)"** is a written statement for each child with a [disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disability) that is developed, reviewed, and revised in a meeting in accordance with [federal regulations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Code_of_Federal_Regulations). It is the binding [contract](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contract) that guarantees [FAPE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_Appropriate_Public_Education) is actually delivered.

![The Individualized Education Program is a legally binding document enforced by the strict administrative standards laid out in the Code of Federal Regulations.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Code_of_Federal_Regulations.jpg)

### 3. Least Restrictive Environment (LRE)
[Special education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education) is a service, not a place. The **"[Least Restrictive Environment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Least_restrictive_environment)"** mandate requires that children with [disabilities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disability) be educated with children who are nondisabled to the maximum extent appropriate. 

Under the [Least Restrictive Environment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Least_restrictive_environment) mandate, removal from the regular educational environment occurs *only* if the [disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disability) severity prevents satisfactory education in regular classes with the use of supplementary aids and services. You cannot sequester a child simply because it is more convenient for the school.

### 4. Transition Services
[Education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Education) must lead somewhere. **"Transition services"** are a coordinated set of activities within a results-oriented process focused on improving academic and functional achievement to facilitate a child's movement from school to post-school activities. 

## The Thirteen Federal Disability Categories

The [Individuals with Disabilities Education Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Individuals_with_Disabilities_Education_Act) defines thirteen distinct [disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disability) categories under which children may be eligible for [special education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education) services. Notice a recurring theme: almost every category requires that the condition *adversely affects a child's educational performance*. If an impairment does not disrupt the child's education, it does not warrant an [IEP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Individualized_Education_Program).

### Cognitive and Learning Differences

**[Specific Learning Disability (SLD)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Learning_disability)**
"[Specific learning disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Learning_disability)" means a disorder in one or more of the basic [psychological processes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mental_process) involved in understanding or in using [language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Language), [spoken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speech) or [written](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing). Think of this as an anomaly in the [brain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brain)'s "wiring" for processing information. 
*   **Manifestation:** A [specific learning disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Learning_disability) may manifest itself in the imperfect ability to listen, think, speak, [read](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reading), [write](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing), [spell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spelling), or to do [mathematical calculations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arithmetic).
*   **Inclusions:** [Specific learning disabilities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Learning_disability) include conditions such as perceptual disabilities, [brain injury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brain_injury), [minimal brain dysfunction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minimal_brain_dysfunction), [dyslexia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dyslexia), and [developmental aphasia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Specific_language_impairment).
*   **Exclusions (Highly Testable):** [Specific learning disabilities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Learning_disability) do *not* include learning problems that are primarily the result of [visual](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visual_impairment), [hearing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hearing_loss), or [motor disabilities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motor_disorder); [intellectual disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intellectual_disability) or [emotional disturbance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emotional_and_behavioral_disorders); or environmental, cultural, or economic disadvantage.

![Educational models distinguishing dyslexia from other reading struggles help teams determine if a child meets the specific neurological criteria for an SLD, rather than a general academic delay.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Simple_View_of_Reading_quadrant_visualisation.jpg)

**[Intellectual Disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intellectual_disability)**
"[Intellectual disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intellectual_disability)" means significantly subaverage general intellectual functioning existing concurrently with deficits in [adaptive behavior](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adaptive_behavior). 
*   **Criteria:** An [intellectual disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intellectual_disability) must manifest during the child's developmental period and must adversely affect a child's educational performance to qualify for [special education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education) services.
*   **Historical Context:** The term "[intellectual disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intellectual_disability)" replaced the term "[mental retardation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intellectual_disability)" in federal education, health, and labor laws due to [Rosa's Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rosa%27s_Law) in 2010.

### Neurodevelopmental and Emotional Profiles

**[Autism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autism)**
"[Autism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autism)" is a [developmental disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developmental_disability) significantly affecting verbal and [nonverbal communication](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonverbal_communication) and [social interaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_relation).
*   **Timeline:** The [developmental disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developmental_disability) of [Autism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autism) is generally evident before a child reaches age three.
*   **Characteristics:** Characteristics often associated with [autism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autism) include engaging in repetitive activities and [stereotyped movements](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stereotypy), resistance to environmental change or resistance to change in daily routines, and unusual responses to sensory experiences. 
*   **Criteria & Exclusions:** To qualify for [special education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education) services under the [Autism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autism) category, the [disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disability) must adversely affect a child's educational performance. Crucially, a child does *not* meet the federal definition of [autism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autism) if the child's educational performance is adversely affected primarily because the child has an [emotional disturbance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emotional_and_behavioral_disorders).

![Repetitive play, such as arranging objects in strict sequential order, is a common behavioral characteristic evaluated when considering a student for the Autism category.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Autistic-sweetiepie-boy-with-ducksinarow.jpg)

**[Emotional Disturbance (ED)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emotional_and_behavioral_disorders)**
"[Emotional disturbance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emotional_and_behavioral_disorders)" is a condition exhibiting one or more specific characteristics over a long period of time and to a marked degree that adversely affects a child's educational performance.
*   **The 5 Characteristics (Must exhibit at least one):**
    1. An inability to learn that cannot be explained by intellectual, sensory, or health factors.
    2. An inability to build or maintain satisfactory [interpersonal relationships](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interpersonal_relationship) with peers and teachers.
    3. Exhibiting inappropriate types of behavior or feelings under normal circumstances.
    4. A general pervasive mood of unhappiness or [depression](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Depression_%28mood%29).
    5. A tendency to develop physical [symptoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symptom) or [fears](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fear) associated with personal or school problems.
*   **Nuances:** The federal [emotional disturbance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emotional_and_behavioral_disorders) category *includes* [schizophrenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schizophrenia). However, the [emotional disturbance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emotional_and_behavioral_disorders) category does *not* apply to children who are socially maladjusted, unless it is determined that they also have an [emotional disturbance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emotional_and_behavioral_disorders).

### Physical, Health, and Acquired Conditions

**Other Health Impairment (OHI)**
"Other health impairment" means having limited strength, vitality, or alertness, including a heightened alertness to environmental stimuli. (Notice the paradox: a student with [ADHD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Attention_deficit_hyperactivity_disorder) has heightened alertness to the bird outside the window, which results in limited alertness with respect to the educational environment).
*   **Causes:** An "other health impairment" must be due to chronic or acute health problems such as [asthma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asthma), [attention deficit disorder (ADD)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Attention_deficit_hyperactivity_disorder), [attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Attention_deficit_hyperactivity_disorder), or [diabetes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diabetes).
*   **Impact:** An "other health impairment" must adversely affect a child's educational performance.

**[Orthopedic Impairment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_disability)**
"[Orthopedic impairment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_disability)" means a severe orthopedic impairment that adversely affects a child's educational performance.
*   **Origins:** Orthopedic impairments include impairments caused by a [congenital anomaly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congenital_disorder); impairments caused by [disease](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disease), such as [poliomyelitis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polio) or bone tuberculosis; and impairments from other causes, such as [cerebral palsy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cerebral_palsy), [amputations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amputation), and [fractures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bone_fracture) or [burns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burn) that cause [contractures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contracture).

![An Orthopedic Impairment like Cerebral Palsy must be severe enough to adversely affect educational performance, often requiring orthotic supports or physical therapy to navigate the school environment.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Cerebralparese_Orthese_orthotics.jpg)

**[Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traumatic_brain_injury)**
"[Traumatic brain injury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traumatic_brain_injury)" means an acquired injury to the [brain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brain) caused by an external physical force, resulting in total or partial functional [disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disability) or psychosocial impairment, or both.
*   **Scope:** The [traumatic brain injury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traumatic_brain_injury) category applies to open or [closed head injuries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Closed_head_injury) resulting in impairments in areas such as [cognition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognition), [language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Language), [memory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memory), [attention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Attention), or [reasoning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reason).
*   **Criteria & Exclusions:** A [traumatic brain injury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traumatic_brain_injury) must adversely affect a child's educational performance. The TBI category does *not* apply to brain injuries that are congenital or [degenerative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Degenerative_disease), nor does it apply to brain injuries induced by [birth trauma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Birth_trauma_%28physical%29). It must be an *acquired* injury from an *external* force.

![TBI classifications under IDEA require an acquired injury from an external physical force, such as the ricochet impact of a coup-contrecoup injury, explicitly excluding congenital conditions or birth trauma.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Contrecoup.svg)

### Sensory and Communication Differences

| Category | Defining Federal Characteristics |
| :--- | :--- |
| **[Speech or Language Impairment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speech_and_language_impairment)** | Means a [communication disorder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communication_disorder), such as [stuttering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stuttering), impaired [articulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speech_sound_disorder), a [language impairment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Language_disorder), or a [voice impairment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voice_disorder). Must adversely affect a child's educational performance. |
| **[Visual Impairment including Blindness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visual_impairment)** | Means an impairment in vision that, even with correction, adversely affects a child's educational performance. The category includes *both* [partial sight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low_vision) and [blindness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blindness). |
| **[Deafness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hearing_loss)** | Is a hearing impairment that is so severe that the child is impaired in processing linguistic information through hearing, with or without [amplification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hearing_aid). Must adversely affect educational performance. |
| **[Hearing Impairment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hearing_loss)** | Means an impairment in hearing, whether permanent or fluctuating, that adversely affects a child's educational performance. *Note:* A hearing impairment is not included under the federal definition of [deafness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hearing_loss). |

### Complex Combinations

When a student has multiple overlapping needs, the federal framework relies on two specific categories to ensure adequate programming.

**[Deaf-Blindness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deafblindness)**
"[Deaf-blindness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deafblindness)" involves concomitant hearing and visual impairments. The combination of impairments in [deaf-blindness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deafblindness) causes severe communication and other developmental and educational needs. The defining legal threshold is that the severe needs caused by [deaf-blindness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deafblindness) cannot be accommodated in [special education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education) programs designed solely for children with [deafness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hearing_loss) or solely for children with [blindness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blindness).

**[Multiple Disabilities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiple_disabilities)**
"[Multiple disabilities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiple_disabilities)" means concomitant impairments, the combination of which causes such severe educational needs that they cannot be accommodated in [special education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_education) programs solely for one of the impairments.
*   **The Exception:** The [multiple disabilities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiple_disabilities) category does *not* include [deaf-blindness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deafblindness). [Deaf-blindness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deafblindness) is entirely its own recognized category.

## The Flexible Provision: Developmental Delay

[Evaluating](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Educational_assessment) very young children is scientifically complex; pinning a permanent label on a four-year-old can be counterproductive. Therefore, IDEA provides a buffer.

"[Developmental delay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developmental_delay)" is an optional category that [states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/U.S._state) and local educational agencies may use for children ages three through nine who are experiencing developmental delays in one or more areas. The developmental areas for the "[developmental delay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developmental_delay)" category include physical, cognitive, communication, social or emotional, or [adaptive development](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adaptive_behavior). 

Because it is an optional category, you must be familiar with whether your specific state and Local Educational Agency (LEA) have adopted it. If they have, it provides an invaluable mechanism to deliver necessary [early interventions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Early_childhood_intervention) without prematurely locking a child into a specific, lifelong [diagnostic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medical_diagnosis) category.

Understanding these classifications is not merely about passing an [exam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Test_%28assessment%29). When you sit at an [IEP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Individualized_Education_Program) table, knowing the distinction between an [Orthopedic Impairment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_disability) and a [Multiple Disability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiple_disabilities), or understanding the precise boundary of an SLD, is what allows you to engineer the right educational [bridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bridge) for the child waiting on the other side.
