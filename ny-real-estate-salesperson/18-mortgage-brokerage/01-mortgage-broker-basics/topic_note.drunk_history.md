And who they borrow it from dictates whether you’re popping champagne at the closing table or sobbing into your steering wheel on the FDR Drive. We are talking about the architects of capital: the Mortgage Brokers and the Mortgage Bankers. 

Imagine a mortgage broker as the ultimate, hyper-connected VIP matchmaker in a reality TV dating show for money. A mortgage broker acts as an intermediary between prospective borrowers and mortgage lenders. They don’t actually have any money themselves to lend, just like a matchmaker isn’t the one marrying your client! Instead, a mortgage broker evaluates a borrower's financial profile to identify suitable loan programs. They put your buyer under the microscope. Oh, your buyer is a freelance mime with a trust fund and terrible credit? The broker knows exactly who will date them. 

To make this match, a mortgage broker collects financial documentation from a borrower to build a loan application file. They are hoarding W-2s, tax returns, and bank statements like a squirrel hoarding nuts before a blizzard. Once that golden binder of truth is assembled, a mortgage broker submits complete loan files to prospective lenders for underwriting approval. They slide that file across the desk to the big institutions and say, "Look at my beautiful client, who wants them?" 

Because they are shopping your client around town, a mortgage broker typically offers access to a wider variety of loan products from multiple lenders than a single direct mortgage banker. They aren’t locked into one menu; they can order from any restaurant in the city! 

But here is the absolute defining limitation of the matchmaker: a mortgage broker does not use proprietary funds to finance mortgage loans. Their pockets are completely empty when it comes to the actual loan amount. They are strictly the hype man! Because of this, a mortgage broker earns compensation through a fee or commission upon the successful closing of a loan. The second the ink dries and the deal is done, the broker evaporates into thin air. Poof! Gone! A mortgage broker does not retain servicing rights for a mortgage loan after the loan closes. They are never going to text your client a year later asking for a monthly mortgage payment. Their job ended at the altar!

Now, pivot your brain. Look across the room at the Mortgage Banker. The banker isn't the matchmaker; the banker is the wealthy aristocrat looking for a partner. A mortgage banker acts as a direct lender for real estate transactions. They don't introduce you to anybody; a mortgage banker originates mortgage loans directly with borrowers. It’s a one-on-one relationship from day one. 

Because it’s their own institution, they make the rules of engagement. You don't have to wonder what some mysterious third party wants, because a mortgage banker establishes internal underwriting guidelines for approving borrower loan applications. They are the judge, jury, and executioner of the loan file. Where do they get the \$800,000 to hand over at closing? A mortgage banker funds mortgage loans using internal capital. They have massive corporate vaults. But even billionaires use leverage, right? So if they don't want to drain their liquid cash, a mortgage banker funds mortgage loans using warehouse lines of credit—think of it as an insanely massive, short-term institutional credit card that they swipe to fund the loan before selling the debt off later to pay back the line. 

And unlike the matchmaking broker who ghosts after the wedding, the banker wants a long-term relationship. A mortgage banker frequently retains the right to service a loan by collecting monthly payments after closing. Your buyer is going to be writing checks to that banker until their kids are in college!

Now, you think you can just wake up tomorrow, print a business card, and start doing this? Absolutely not. Welcome to the bouncer at the door of our little casino: the State of New York. Because of the sheer economic firepower involved here, the New York State Department of Financial Services regulates mortgage brokers. And don't think the big guys get a pass, because the New York State Department of Financial Services regulates mortgage bankers, too! They watch everyone! 

But the state is smart; they tailor the security pat-down to the threat level. The banker is moving millions in actual corporate capital, so New York State requires mortgage bankers to hold a formal license from the Department of Financial Services. A full-blown, heavy-duty license. The broker, however, is just an intermediary shuffling files. So, New York State requires mortgage brokers to obtain a registration from the Department of Financial Services. 

But do not let the word "registration" make you think it's a walk in the park! The state doesn't want idiot matchmakers ruining people's financial lives. A New York mortgage broker applicant must demonstrate prior experience in the business of residential mortgage loans. You have to prove you’ve been in the trenches! And if you mess up? If you drop the ball and shatter a buyer’s dream? The New York State Department of Financial Services requires registered mortgage brokers to maintain a surety bond. It's a massive financial safety net, a pile of emergency cash sitting there just in case the broker commits a catastrophic blunder and the consumer needs to be made whole.

Speaking of consumer protection, the state is hyper-paranoid about brokers nickel-and-diming buyers in the dark. Let's say a broker says, "Sure, I can find you a loan, just Venmo me a \$500 application fee right now so I can pull your credit." BAM! Illegal! Halt! A mortgage broker must provide a borrower with a written fee agreement before accepting any application fees. The state demands absolute transparency. The buyer must see the menu and the prices in writing before they hand over a single dime for the matchmaking service.
