Think about playing a multiplayer video game. If you and your friend agree to trade a super rare sword for 500 gold coins, you expect them to keep their promise, right? In the adult world, especially when buying big things like houses, people use special agreements. A contract is a legally enforceable agreement between competent parties to perform or abstain from a specific act. This means the law will make sure people do what they promised, or stay away from doing something they promised not to do. When someone spends a lot of money to buy property, the contract is the ultimate rulebook that makes sure nobody gets cheated.

## The Form of Agreement: Express vs. Implied

Before we can use a rulebook, we have to agree on what the rules are. We usually do this in two ways: by talking (or writing) and by acting. 

An express contract is an agreement where the parties explicitly state the exact terms and conditions. It's like saying, "I will trade you my skateboard if you give me your bike." There is no guessing involved because the rules are clear. You might think this has to be on paper, but an express contract can be created through either oral statements or written documents. If someone says, "I will let you live in this apartment for \$2,000 a month," and you say, "I accept," you have made an express contract just by speaking!

But sometimes people don't use words at all. An implied contract is an agreement created by the actions and conduct of the parties involved. Imagine walking into a pizza shop, pointing at a slice of cheese pizza, and the worker hands it to you. You didn't officially say "I promise to pay you," but your actions created an agreement that you would pay for the food.

## The Symmetry of Promises: Bilateral vs. Unilateral

Contracts are basically traded promises. But how those promises are traded changes what kind of contract it is. 

### Bilateral Contracts
The prefix "bi-" means two, like a bicycle. A bilateral contract is an agreement where both parties make a binding promise to perform a specific action. It is a two-way street: I promise to do something for you, and you promise to do something for me.

> **Real Estate Application:** A standard real estate sales contract is an example of a bilateral contract. The buyer promises to pay money, and the seller promises to hand over the house. Both people are making a legal promise at the exact same time.

### Unilateral Contracts
The prefix "uni-" means one, like a unicycle. A unilateral contract is an agreement where only one party makes a binding promise to perform an action. It is a one-way street! The second person doesn't have to do anything, but if they *choose* to do it, the first person is forced to keep their promise.

> **Real Estate Application:** 
> 1. An open listing agreement is a common example of a unilateral real estate contract. The person selling the house promises to pay an agent a bonus *only if* the agent finds a buyer. The agent doesn't promise they will find someone, but if they do, the seller has to pay up.
> 2. An option contract to purchase real estate is a unilateral contract. The seller promises to hold the house for the buyer for a certain time, but the buyer doesn't have to buy it if they change their mind. 

| Feature | Bilateral Contract | Unilateral Contract |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mechanic** | Promise for a Promise | Promise for an Act |
| **Obligation** | Both parties are legally bound immediately. | Only the party making the promise is bound. |
| **Example** | Standard Property Sales Contract | Open Listing Agreement, Option Contract |

## The Four Pillars of a Valid Contract

For a judge to say a contract is good and fair, it needs four main parts. Think of these like the four legs of a table. If one is missing, the table falls over.

### 1. Legal Competency
First, the essentials of a valid contract include legally competent parties. This means both people have to be ready and able to make a big decision. 

What does that mean? Legal competency requires a party to possess sufficient mental capacity to understand the terms of the contract. You have to know exactly what you are agreeing to. Also, legal competency requires a party to be of legal age to enter into a contract. Kids can't sell houses! In New York State, the legal age of majority to enter into a binding contract is 18 years old.

### 2. Mutual Agreement
Second, the essentials of a valid contract include mutual agreement between the parties. You both have to agree on the exact same thing. Mutual agreement is often referred to in contract law as a meeting of the minds.

To get this perfect meeting of the minds, two things must happen:
*   Mutual agreement requires a clear offer made by one party. ("I will buy your video game console for \$200.")
*   Mutual agreement requires an unconditional acceptance of the offer by the receiving party. ("Yes, I accept.") If they say "Yes, but only if you pay \$250," that is not unconditional. The minds haven't met!

### 3. Lawful Objective
Third, the essentials of a valid contract include a lawful objective. You cannot make a contract to do something illegal. A contract with an unlawful objective is legally void from its inception. (Inception means the very beginning). If two people sign a paper agreeing to rob a bank, the judge will just throw it in the trash.

### 4. Consideration
Fourth, the essentials of a valid contract include consideration. Consideration refers to anything of value exchanged between parties to legally bind a contract. This shows it is a real trade, not just a free gift. 

Consideration comes in two types:
*   Valuable consideration in a contract typically consists of money or equivalent tangible assets. (Like paying \$500 or trading a car).
*   Good consideration can consist of love and affection between family members. (Like a mom giving a family house to her son just because she loves him. The law says love counts as a fair trade between family!).

## The New York Statute of Frauds

Sometimes people forget what they promised, which leads to huge arguments. To stop fights over big deals, there is a special rule called the Statute of Frauds. It says some agreements are so important they MUST be written down.

*   The New York Statute of Frauds requires all contracts for the sale of real estate to be in writing to be legally enforceable. 
*   Also, the New York Statute of Frauds requires real estate leases exceeding a term of one year to be in writing. If you rent an apartment for just six months, you can do it by just talking. But if it's for over a year, grab a pen!

## Contract Deficiencies: Void, Voidable, and Unenforceable

What if a contract is broken or messed up? It falls into one of three buckets. 

### The Void Contract
A void contract lacks at least one essential element of a valid contract (like someone being too young, or trading for something illegal). Because it's missing a crucial piece, a void contract has no legal force or binding effect on any party. It is like a video game that won't even turn on. Legally, it never existed at all.

### The Voidable Contract
A voidable contract is tricky because it looks perfectly fine on the outside. A voidable contract is legally binding unless disaffirmed by the party holding the specific legal right to rescind the agreement. (Rescind means to hit the eject button and cancel it). This usually happens to protect someone who was taken advantage of.

> **Real Estate Application:** 
> *   A contract entered into by a minor is generally voidable by that minor. If a 16-year-old signs a lease for an apartment, the adult landlord is stuck with the rules, but the 16-year-old has the legal right to cancel it and walk away.
> *   Also, a contract entered into under duress is voidable by the coerced party. (Duress means someone bullied or forced you into signing). If a bully forces you to trade your lunch, you have the right to cancel that trade!

### The Unenforceable Contract
An unenforceable contract is an agreement that a court will not compel the parties to fulfill. The agreement might have all four pillars, but it broke a specific rule.

> **Real Estate Application:** An oral contract for the sale of real estate is typically unenforceable due to the Statute of Frauds. If you and your neighbor shake hands on a \$1,000,000 house sale and your neighbor backs out, the judge won't force them to sell. Even though you agreed, it wasn't in writing, so the court simply ignores it.

## The Timeline of Performance: Executory vs. Executed

Finally, contracts happen over time, just like baking a pizza.

An executory contract is an agreement where one or more parties have not yet completed their required obligations. The pizza is still in the oven! You signed the paper to buy the house, but you haven't handed over the money yet, and the seller hasn't packed their boxes. The promises have been made, but the work is still happening.

An executed contract is an agreement where all parties have fully performed their required obligations. The pizza is delivered and eaten! You paid the money, the seller gave you the keys, and everyone is happy. The contract's job is totally complete.
