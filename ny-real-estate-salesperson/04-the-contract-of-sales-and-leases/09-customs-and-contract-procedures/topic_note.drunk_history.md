Now, let’s talk about the actual standoff. The signing. It’s like a hostage exchange in a heist movie—who hands over the bag first? In a New York real estate transaction, the buyer signs the contract of sale first. Period. And they don't just sign an autograph; they have to drop the briefcase of cash. The buyer submits the earnest money down payment at the exact time of signing the contract of sale. Why this exact sequence? Imagine the seller taking their house off the market, turning away ten other desperate buyers, just because some guy *said* he'd buy it. Absolute madness! The buyer signing first ensures the seller has a financially backed offer before officially taking the property off the market. The seller needs to see the skin in the game. Once that money is on the table, and only then, the seller signs the contract of sale after the buyer signs the document. 

You think you're done? You think because both people scribbled their names, the deal is alive? Rookie mistake!
