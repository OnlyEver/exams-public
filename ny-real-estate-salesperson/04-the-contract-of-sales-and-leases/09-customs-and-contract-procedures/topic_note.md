A [real estate transaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_estate_transaction) in [New York](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_%28state%29) is governed by a precise choreography of legal and financial commitments. The moment a [meeting of the minds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meeting_of_the_minds) transforms into a written agreement, the roles of the [buyer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buyer), the [seller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seller), their respective [attorneys](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lawyer), and the [real estate licensee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_estate_broker) lock into a strict sequence of procedures. Understanding the exact order in which documents are drafted, reviewed, signed, and delivered is not merely a matter of administrative housekeeping; it is the fundamental architecture that dictates when a property is officially removed from the market, how [financial risk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Financial_risk) is mitigated, and when a licensee’s [fiduciary duty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiduciary) translates into a binding, executable deal.

## The Anatomy of Drafting: Architects and Orchestrators

As a [real estate professional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_estate_broker), you are the orchestrator of the deal, but you are not its legal architect. To ensure transactions remain legally sound, New York draws a bright line between facilitating a sale and [practicing law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Practice_of_law). 

In practice, **the seller's attorney customarily drafts the [real estate contract](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_estate_contract) of sale in New York**. The seller's [counsel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lawyer) holds the responsibility of outlining the foundational terms of the [property transfer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conveyancing). However, the modern [real estate market](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_estate_market) requires speed and efficiency. To facilitate this, **[real estate brokers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_estate_broker) in New York are permitted to fill in the blanks on standardized real estate contracts**, such as those approved by local [bar associations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bar_association) or real estate boards. 

Your authority, however, ends strictly at those blank lines. 

> **Warning:** A real estate broker is strictly prohibited from drafting a real estate contract of sale from scratch. Doing so crosses a fundamental legal boundary; drafting a real estate contract from scratch by a real estate broker constitutes the **[unauthorized practice of law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unauthorized_practice_of_law)**.

Furthermore, while clients will inevitably look to you for guidance when reviewing these documents, **a real estate broker cannot provide [legal advice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_advice) regarding the terms of a real estate contract**. Your role is to negotiate the business terms—such as a \$950,000 [purchase price](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Price) or a specific [closing date](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Closing_%28real_estate%29)—and transmit those terms to the attorneys for formalization. 

### The Attorney Review Safety Net

Because standardized real estate contracts filled out by brokers involve non-lawyers outlining [legally binding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contract) terms, the law requires a built-in safety mechanism. Standardized real estate contracts filled out by brokers must contain an **attorney review clause**. 

An attorney review clause makes the real estate contract subject to the approval of the buyer's attorney and the seller's attorney. Once the document is signed, an attorney review period typically lasts for **three [business days](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Business_day) after the real estate contract is signed**. 

During this window, either attorney can halt the transaction if the standard terms expose their client to undue risk. If an attorney finds the terms legally unacceptable, the mechanism is absolute: **a real estate contract becomes [null and void](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Void_%28law%29) upon disapproval by either attorney during the attorney review period.**

| Role | Permitted Actions | Prohibited Actions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Real Estate Broker** | Negotiate business terms; fill in blanks on pre-printed standard [contracts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contract). | Draft contracts from scratch; provide legal advice on contract terms; draft custom riders. |
| **Seller's Attorney** | Draft the main contract of sale; review broker-prepared standard contracts. | N/A (Operates within full legal authority). |
| **Buyer's Attorney** | Review, approve, or [void](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Void_%28law%29) standard contracts during the review period; draft custom riders. | N/A (Operates within full legal authority). |

## The Choreography of Signatures

When a contract is ready to be formalized, the sequence in which parties sign is heavily regulated by custom and [risk management](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Risk_management). 

In a New York real estate transaction, **the buyer signs the contract of sale first**. Simultaneously, **the buyer submits the [earnest money](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earnest_payment) [down payment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Down_payment) at the exact time of signing the contract of sale**. Suppose your buyer is offering \$50,000 as earnest money; that [check](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cheque) or [wire transfer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wire_transfer) must accompany their signature. 

Why this specific sequence? It is a mechanism of market protection. **The buyer signing first ensures the seller has a financially backed offer before officially taking the property off the market.** If a seller were to sign first, they would effectively be granting the buyer a free, binding option to purchase the property without any financial skin in the game, paralyzing the seller's ability to entertain other offers. Consequently, **the seller signs the contract of sale after the buyer signs the document.**

### Execution versus Delivery

Signing the document is only part of the physics of a contract. You must understand the distinction between a contract being *executed* and a contract being *binding*.

1. **Execution:** **A real estate contract is not fully executed until both the buyer and the seller have signed the document.** 
2. **Binding Status:** Even with both signatures, the contract remains in a state of suspended animation. **A real estate contract is not binding until the fully signed contract is delivered to the buyer or the buyer's attorney.** Delivery is the ignition switch; without it, the transaction does not officially begin.

## Contract Riders: Customizing the Deal

Standard contracts are highly effective for standard transactions, but no two properties or clients are identical. When a transaction requires specific [contingencies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contingency_%28contract%29)—such as a complex seller concession or a post-closing possession agreement—the standard form is insufficient. 

To accommodate this, attorneys use a **[rider](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rider_%28contract%29)**, which is an attachment added to a real estate contract to include additional terms or conditions. 

Just as with the primary contract, the drafting of these attachments is heavily restricted. **Only licensed attorneys are permitted to draft custom riders for real estate contracts in New York.** Consequently, **real estate licensees are strictly prohibited from drafting custom contract riders.** 

When a rider is attached to a standard contract, it carries ultimate legal weight. Because the rider represents the specific, customized [intent of the parties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intention_to_create_legal_relations) for this unique transaction, **terms within a customized contract rider supersede conflicting terms within the standard printed contract.** 

## Environmental Mandates: The Lead-Based Paint Clause

New York possesses an aging housing stock, which introduces vital [environmental compliance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_compliance) steps into the contracting procedure. Chief among these is the [federal regulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Code_of_Federal_Regulations) regarding [lead-based paint](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lead_paint). 

![Volumes of the Code of Federal Regulations, which codify the strict environmental compliance and active disclosure mandates required for older residential properties.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Code_of_Federal_Regulations.jpg)

Under [federal law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_law_of_the_United_States), **a lead-based paint disclosure must be attached to the sales contract for [residential properties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Residential_area) constructed before 1978.** This is not merely an advisory warning; it requires active disclosure. **The lead-based paint disclosure requires the seller to report any known lead-based paint hazards in the property.**

![Cracking and flaking lead-based paint creates severe health hazards, necessitating the mandatory 10-day inspection period granted to buyers of pre-1978 homes.](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Redirect/file/Lead_Paint_-_cracking_and_flaking.jpg)

Upon receiving this disclosure, **federal law grants buyers of pre-1978 residential properties a 10-day period to conduct a lead-based paint inspection.** This ensures the buyer can accurately assess the environmental risk before finalizing their purchase. However, the law recognizes the buyer's [autonomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autonomy) in this matter: **buyers have the right to [waive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waiver) the 10-day lead-based paint inspection period in writing.**

To enforce strict adherence to these environmental protections, the paperwork requires comprehensive execution:
* **Both the buyer and the seller must sign the lead-based paint disclosure form.**
* Crucially, accountability extends directly to the professionals facilitating the transaction. **Real estate agents involved in the transaction must sign the lead-based paint disclosure form to acknowledge [compliance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regulatory_compliance).** 

Failing to properly execute the lead-based paint disclosure does not just jeopardize the transaction; it exposes the seller and the agents to severe federal penalties. In real estate, precision in documentation is exactly what transforms an abstract agreement into a secure, closed deal.
