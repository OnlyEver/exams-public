Because of this, intense and aggressive solicitation practices by real estate agents in specific neighborhoods can lead to the creation of geographic cease and desist zones. Picture a cease and desist zone like a giant, invisible dome over a neighborhood. But inside that dome, it's not an automatic lockdown. It's an opt-in forcefield. To activate their shield, homeowners located within a designated cease and desist zone must file an owner's statement with the Department of State to be placed on the cease and desist list. Once they file that paperwork, a New York State cease and desist list contains the exact names and addresses of homeowners who have formally requested no real estate solicitations. This list is your personal minefield. Real estate licensees are legally prohibited from soliciting any residential property listing from owners whose addresses appear on the active cease and desist list. That means no knocking! But here is the trick to surviving the minefield: the dome has holes. Licensees are permitted to solicit homeowners located inside a cease and desist zone if those specific homeowners have not registered on the cease and desist list.
